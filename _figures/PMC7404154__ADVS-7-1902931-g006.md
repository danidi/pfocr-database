caption: 'Synergy between cytoskeletal tension (YAP/TAZ) and the BMP‐2‐Smad pathway.
  Left: No activated Smad signaling or osteogenic differentiation is observed in the
  absence of BMP‐2, even in the presence of stiff matrix. Right: The initiation of
  BMP‐2‐induced Smad signaling is independent of cytoskeletal tension. Smad1/5/8 can
  be phosphorylated and form heteromeric complexes that translocate into the nucleus
  and bind to target genes. However, osteogenic gene activation requires cytoskeletal
  tension‐induced nuclear accumulation of YAP/TAZ. Thus, BMP‐2 signaling responds
  to mechanical cues by sensing nucleocytoplasmic shuttling of YAP/TAZ.'
