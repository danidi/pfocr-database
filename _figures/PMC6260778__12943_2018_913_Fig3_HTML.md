caption: Effect of glycolysis on Tregs. CD28 on the surface of Tregs is activated
  to promote GCK expression via PI3K/mTORC2, GCK, and actin, which can promote cytoskeletal
  remodeling, and GCK can promote glycolysis to provide energy. Upon stimulation of
  TLR1/2 or IL-2R on the Treg surface, activation of the PI3K/AKT/mTORC1 signaling
  pathway promotes Glut1 expression on the cell membrane surface and promotes glycolysis
  to promote proliferation. Akt inhibits the function of Tregs by inhibiting the expression
  of Foxp3, which in turn inhibits the transcription factor Foxo1/3. Foxp3 inhibits
  glycolysis by inhibiting the signaling pathway of PI3K/AKT/mTORC1 and the expression
  of Myc. AMPK in Tregs inhibits glycolysis by suppressing mTORC1
