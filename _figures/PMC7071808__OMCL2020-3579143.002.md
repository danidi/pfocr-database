caption: Oxidative stress in TGF-β1-mediated fibrosis. Radiation induces reactive
  oxygen species (ROS), and ROS can activate TGF-β1, resulting in the generation of
  thrombin and the activation of platelets to promote further production of ROS and
  TGF-β1. TGF-β1 binds to Type I (TbRI) and Type II (TbRII) transmembrane receptors,
  activating the Smad pathway and the TAK1/MKK3/p38 pathway through the specific nuclear
  signaling transduction molecule, TRAF6. The Smad pathway requires a number of inflammatory
  cytokines, in particular IL-6 and IL-8, to mediate collagen synthesis. TGF-β1 activates
  the TAK1/MKK3/p38 signaling pathway and downregulates autophagy-mediated collagen
  degradation, which may further promote the synthesis of collagen, form a fibrotic
  environment, cause atherosclerosis and ischemic heart disease, and may even lead
  to heart failure.
