caption: Calcium and strontium induce distinct intracelular calcium signaling. Upon
  stimulation by extracellular calcium, calcium-sensing receptor (CaR) activates PLC,
  which is responsible for the translocation of nuclear factor κB (NF-κB) from the
  cytoplasm to the nucleus in mature osteoclasts, in an IP3-dependent manner. Upon
  stimulation by extracellular strontium, CaR also activates PLC inducing the DAG-PKCβII
  signaling pathway and then promoting translocation of NF-κB from the cytoplasm to
  the nucleus in an IP3-independent manner. Taken from [].
