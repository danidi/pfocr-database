caption: Schematic representation of the proposed PTN pro-migratory signaling pathway
  that leads to CDK5 activation in human endothelial cells. PTN induces RPTPβ/ζ stimulation
  that triggers an RPTPβ/ζ-p35-CDK5 interaction. This event leads to CDK5 activation
  and is independent of the PTN-induced migratory signals through integrin ανβ3. Furthermore,
  RPTPβ/ζ stimulation may lead to CDK5 activation through a PI3K-dependent manner.
  Finally, we show that Rac1 lies downstream of CDK5 and is necessary for PTN-induced
  endothelial cell migration.
