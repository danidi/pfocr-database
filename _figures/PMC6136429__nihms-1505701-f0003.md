caption: Chromosome segregation errors lead to the formation of micronuclei, which
  often rupture in S-phase exposing double-stranded DNA (dsDNA) to the cytosol. The
  presence of cytosolic dsDNA activates the anti-viral cGAS-STING pathway which in
  normal cells promotes type I interferon production (A). Chromosomally unstable cancer
  cells however largely suppress type I interferon signaling through multiple mechanisms,
  yet they maintain the ability to exhibit alternative inflammatory STING-dependent
  signaling such as NF-κB (B). Chronic NF-κB activation has been shown to mediate
  the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP) as well as cellular migration
  and metastasis.
