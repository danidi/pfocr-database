  levels of atRA, whose signaling regulates developmental genes (e.g., Hox1) through
  nuclear receptors (e.g., RAR and PPAR). The enzymes of the canonical pathway (in
  bold: Rdh10, Aldh1a, and Cyp26) regulate the synthesis of atRA from atROL (vitamin
  A) to atRAL precursors. In vertebrates, the RA-MGN is a robust system due to the
  presence of other redundant enzymes that bypass the canonical pathway (i.e., RdhE2,
  Rdh16, Adh1/4, Aldh8a1, and Cco).'
