caption: The pathway involving the conversion of glucose to fatty acids (FAs) begins
  with citrate metabolism. In health, citrate is fully oxidized into the Krebs cycle
  to generate ATP. In tumour cells, citrate is preferentially transported to the cytoplasm,
  where it is converted into acetyl-CoA, which constitutes the building block of FAs.
  Complex FAs, in turn, tend to partition into detergent-resistant membrane microdomains
  called lipid rafts, which have a role in cell membranes genesis and signalling molecules
  regulation.Key molecule in this metabolic switch-essential for tumour cells survival-is
  FASN, the enzyme that catalyzes the first step of de novo synthesis of FAs.
