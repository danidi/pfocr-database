caption: Neoplastic transformation of melanocyte. Upon mutation in BRAF or NRAS gene,
  melanocyte acquires the ability to sustained proliferation. Subsequent mutation
  in the promotor of TERT enables the cell to avoid senescence while inhibition of
  RB and p53 pathways suppresses cell cycle control and apoptosis. At this point melanocyte
  turns into melanoma cell. Further activation of PI3K/AKT pathway and involvement
  of other tumour-related proteins (e.g. MITF, WNT, STAT3, NOTCH) regulates such processes
  as invasive growth, induction of angiogenesis and immunosuppression. Finally, all
  the aforementioned molecular changes lead to development of aggressive, metastatic
  melanoma cell
