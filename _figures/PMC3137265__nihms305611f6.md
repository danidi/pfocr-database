caption: Convergence of mTORC1 and GSK3-β in LPS-stimulated cells. LPS stimulation
  induces PI3K/Akt activity, which, in turn, mediates activation of the mTORC1 pathway.
  Activation of mTORC1 results in the phosphorylation and activation of p85S6K. Activated
  p85S6K binds with and phosphorylates GSK3-β, resulting in the suppression of GSK3-β
  activity. Suppressed GSK3-β activity enhances the nuclear binding levels of CREB
  that promote IL-10 and decrease IL-12 production. The phosphorylation of STAT3 in
  LPS-stimulated cells depends upon IL-10.
