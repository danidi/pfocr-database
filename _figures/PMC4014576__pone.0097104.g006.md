caption: Active Rb/E2F complexes bind to E2F sites within the promoters of several
  pro-apoptotic genes, repressing their transcription. Treatment of the U2OS ER-dnE2F1
  cell line with 4-OHT results in the translocation of dnE2F1 to the nucleus where
  it displaces Rb/E2F complexes from their binding sites. The loss of Rb/E2F repression
  leads to the up-regulation of PTP-1B, SHP-2, and PTEN (and potentially other phosphatases),
  dephosphorylation of FAK, and stimulation of apoptosis through activation of caspase-8.
