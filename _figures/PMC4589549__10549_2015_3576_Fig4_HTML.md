caption: Canonical Wnt pathway illustrating the roles of PITX2 dependant genes. Solid
  lines indicate activated state and discontinuous lines indicated inhibited state
  of the pathway. Genes highlighted in yellow shapes showed significant reduction
  upon PITX2 downregulation. When activated by the Wnt ligands, the Frizzled/LRP1/6
  receptor complex inhibits the GSK3/Axin/APC complex and stabilizes beta-Catenin.
  Subsequently beta-Catenin facilitates the nuclear translocation of TCF/LEF complex
  resulting in the transactivation of target genes including PITX2, NKD1, DKK4, and
  several other genes contributing to invasion and metastasis. NKD1 antagonizes the
  Wnt pathway as transcriptional repressor and also by mediating the degradation of
  DVL1
