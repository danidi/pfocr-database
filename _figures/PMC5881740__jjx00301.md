caption: The JAK-STAT signalling pathway. Many cytokines important in the pathogenesis
  of inflammatory bowel disease [IBD] signal via the JAK-STAT pathway. Cytokine signalling
  induces phosphorylation of JAKs, which phosphorylate STAT proteins. STAT proteins
  form homo- or heterodimers and migrate into the nucleus where they activate transcription
  of inflammatory cytokines. JAK inhibitors are new therapeutic agents currently being
  investigated in clinical trials in IBD. First-generation JAK inhibitors [e.g. tofacitinib]
  target multiple JAKs, whereas second-generation JAK inhibitors [e.g. filgotinib]
  selectively target one JAK.
