caption: The principal protein-protein interactions that regulate hTERT involve cross-regulation
  between hTERT and the cell cycle, carried out largely by cyclin/cdk complexes and
  transcription factors that bind to the hTERT promoter. The cyclic nature of the
  cell cycle is reflected in the dual role of E2F-1 as both repressor and activator.
  Major regulatory pathways exert either repressive, as in the case of the TGF-b pathway,
  or activating influence on both the cell cycle and hTERT expression. The PI3K/Akt,
  NF-kB and MAP kinase pathways all activate. In addition, estrogen (E2) bound to
  estrogen receptor ERa blocks the repressive activity of the TGF-b pathway, as does
  cyclin/cdk phosphorylation of p107. In a positive feedback loop, expression of hTERT
  activates the PI3K/Akt pathway, which activates the cell cycle by MAD1 and p53 degradation,
  activates the NF-kB pathway and potentially blocks the TGF-b pathway. Arrow shapeMeaningDeltaIncrease,
