caption: Overview of the sulfate assimilation pathway in metazoans, yeast, and plants.
  The boxed numbers represent the various enzyme-catalyzed reactions and the genes
  that encode for the activities from the representative species M. Musculus, S. Cerevisiae,
  and A. Thaliana. Of note, yeast are capable of incorporating inorganic sulfate into
  sulfur-containing amino acids while metazoans instead utilize sulfate for the modification
  of small molecules, lipids, and proteoglycans. Plants contain the enzymes necessary
  for both aspects of the pathway.
