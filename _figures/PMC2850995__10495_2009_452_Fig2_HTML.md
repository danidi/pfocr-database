caption: Lysosomal participation in cell death signaling. Lysosomal membrane permeabilization
  (LMP), i.e., release of cathepsins from lysosomes into the cytosol, is an important
  step in cell death signaling induced by a variety of stimuli, such as death receptor
  activation, radiation, and cytotoxic drugs. Several cytosolic cathepsin targets
  have been described and the signaling downstream LMP involves both caspase-dependent
  and -independent pathways. Engagement of the mitochondrial pathway is a common downstream
  event of LMP, but cathepsins may also cause cell death without involvement of mitochondria
