caption: Synthetic scheme of CS oligosaccharides using enzymes. Panel A shows the
  scheme for the synthesis of chondroitin backbones. Panel B shows the conversion
  of backbone to CS-A targets. Panel C shows the pathway for synthesis of CS-C oligosaccharides.
  Step a is an elongation step to add a GalNAc residue involving KfoC, a glycosyltransferase
  from E. coli K4. Step b is also an elongation step to add a GlcA residue using KfoC.
  Step c is a 6-O-sulfation step involving chondroitin sulfate 6-O-sulfotransferase
  (CS6OST) and sulfate donor 3′-phosphoadenosine 5′-phosphosulfate (PAPS). Step d
  is a 4-O-sulfation step involving chondroitin sulfate 4-O-sulfotransferase (CS4OST)
  and PAPS. KfoC is also able to transfer a GalNTFA residue to a trisaccharide backbone
  to yield a tetrasaccharide, where the trifluoroacetyl group (TFA) is in red color.
  The yield from each reaction step is indicated. ISAH represents imidazole-1-sulfonyl
  azide.
