caption: Under normoxic conditions, hydroxylation at 2 proline residues by prolyl
  hydroxylases (PHDs) promotes HIF-α association with VHL and leads to HIF-α destruction
  via the ubiquitin/proteasome pathway. In hypoxia, these processes are suppressed,
  allowing HIF-α subunits (both HIF-1α and HIF-2α) to escape proteolysis, dimerize
  with HIF-1β, translocate to the nucleus, and activate transcription via hypoxia-response
  element (HREs). HIF activation by PHD inhibitors, conservative oxygenation, ischemic
  preconditioning (IPC), and remote ischemic preconditioning (RIPC) can be potential
  therapeutic approaches for perioperative organ injury.
