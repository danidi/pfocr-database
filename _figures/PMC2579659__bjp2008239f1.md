caption: 'Regulation of smooth muscle cell contraction. Vascular smooth muscle contraction
  is determined primarily by the balance in activities of Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent
  myosin light chain kinase (MLCK, contraction) and Ca2+-independent MLC phosphatase
  (MLCP, relaxation). G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) agonists (ET-1, endothelin-1;
  5-HT, serotonin; TXA2, thromboxane A2; and so on) not only activate MLCK, but also
  inactivate MLCP by activation of RhoA/Rho kinase pathway and/or activation of the
  MLCP-inhibitor protein CPI-17 (not shown) to cause smooth muscle cell contraction.
  In some instances, Rho kinase activation can also induce Ca2+signalling, and Ca2+
  signals can activate RhoA/Rho kinase. IP3, inositol 1,4,5-triphosphate; PLC; MYPT1,
  regulatory myosin-binding subunit of MLCP (modified from Nagaoka et al. (2005) Am
  J Respir Crit Care Med 171: 494–499).'
