caption: Dehydration alters hippocampal transcriptional networks. A hippocampal transcriptional
  network analysis was performed, comprising next-generation RNA sequencing and bioinformatic
  analyses. (a) Genes that were differentially regulated between the control (CON)
  and dehydrated (DEH) mice were classified into functional categories via an Ingenuity
  Pathway Analysis (IPA). Significantly changed biological functions are indicated
  by p-values calculated using a Fisher’s exact test, and the number of distinctively
  modulated molecules in each functional category is indicated. (b–d) Transcriptional
  networks related to altered glucose metabolism, synaptogenic cell-adhesion, and
  synaptic plasticity were differentially regulated in response to long-term dehydration.
  (e) Genes associated with long-term potentiation (LTP) signaling transduction were
  differentially up- (red) or downregulated (green) in the DEH compared to the CON
  mice.
