caption: Opposing functions for small RNA pathway activity during the antiviral response.
  (A) In uninfected cells, endogenous miRNAs are proposed to repress many transcripts
  that encoded factors involved in viral defense (interferon-stimulated genes, ISGs).
  (B) Ago2 is PARylated following certain cellular stresses, including viral infection.
  As PARylation is thought to inhibit Ago function, this modification may broadly
  derepress ISGs in order to potentiate the antiviral interferon response. On the
  other hand, double-stranded RNA viral intermediates can also generate siRNAs that
  may in turn target the virus (right).
