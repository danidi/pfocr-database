caption: A simplified schematic shows the major intracellular signal transduction
  pathways that regulate the epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) process in
  fibrotic kidneys. EMT is a complex biological process through the TGF-β1, integrin/ILK
  or wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway. These pathways are intricately interconnected
  and converged by activation of β-catenin. TGF-β1 is a chief inducer of the EMT process,
  and p-Smad2/3, p38 MAPK and ILK function as mediators of the TGF-β1 signaling pathway.
  (TGF-β1, transforming growth factor-β1; p-Smad2/3, phosphorylated-Smad2/3; MAPK,
  mitogen-activated protein kinase; GSK-3β, glycogen synthase kinase-3β; ILK, integrin-linked
  kinase; Fzd, frizzled receptors; LRP 5/6, low density lipoprotein receptor-related
  protein 5/6; Dvl, Disheveled; APC, adenomatosis polyposis coli; TCF/LEF-1 complex,
  T cell factor/lymphoid enhancer-binding factor-1 complex).
