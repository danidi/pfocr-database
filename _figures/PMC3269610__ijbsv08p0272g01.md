caption: 'TGF-β signaling and negative regulation in bone formation. Canonical Smad-dependent
  TGF-β signaling first binds to receptor type II (R-II) and receptor type I (R-I),
  and then signaling transduces to their Smads. Activated Smads form a complex with
  Smad4 and then translocate into the nucleus where they interact with other transcription
  factors to trigger target gene expression. Smad7 disrupts the activated Smad2/3
  to form a complex with Smad4. The non-Smad-dependent TAK1 signaling pathway also
  regulates bone formation. PTH binding activates PTH1R to stimulate several downstream
  effectors. PTH binding also drives internalization of PTH1R-TGFβRII complex, which
  attenuates both TGF-β and PTH signaling on bone development. Transcriptional factor
  cAMP response element binding protein (CREB) mediates PTH signaling in osteoblasts.
  P: phosphorylation; Ub: ubiquitination.'
