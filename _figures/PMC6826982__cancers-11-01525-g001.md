caption: PTEN in cancer immune microenvironment regulation. PTEN loss in tumor cells
  induces an immunosuppressive microenvironment through secretion of immunosuppressive
  cytokines and MDSCs/Tregs chemoattractant molecules, inhibition of autophagy pathway
  and CD8 T cell killing, secretion of miRNA-containing exosomes to target PTEN expression
  in MDSCs inducing their activation. PTEN loss in fibroblasts surrounding tumor increases
  immune cell infiltration and induces microenvironment remodeling while inhibition
  of AKT pathway in T cells enhances memory T cell differentiation.
