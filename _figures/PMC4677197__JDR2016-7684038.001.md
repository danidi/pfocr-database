caption: The canonical Wnt signalling pathway implicated in diabetic nephropathy.
  When a WNT ligand binds to the Frizzled (FZD) and the LRP5/6 coreceptors, the Dvl
  protein is recruited and inhibits the binding of β-catenin, leading to an increase
  in cytoplasmic β-catenin levels and subsequent activation of downstream signalling
  targets (a). In the absence of any WNT ligand (b), β-catenin is sequestered by the
  Axin complex containing such proteins as GSK3-β, APC, CK1, βTrCP, and axin itself.
  β-catenin is then phosphorylated and ubiquitinated and enters the proteasomal degradation
  pathway and leading to subsequent repression of downstream signalling targets.
