caption: Mechanism of polyphenols' antioxidant properties. Polyphenols activate Keap1/Nrf2/ARE
  pathway and induce the expression of antioxidant/detoxification enzymes. Keap 1
  protein always bound to Nrf2 transcription regulator and prevents its signaling.
  Polyphenols directly or indirectly cause dissociation of Keap1 from the Nrf2-Keap1
  complex and subsequent translocation of Nrf2 to the nucleus where it binds to the
  ARE in the regulatory region of the target genes and induce transcription of antioxidant/detoxification
  enzymes. ARE, antioxidant response element; CAT, catalase; ERK, extracellular signal-regulated
  kinase; GCL, g-glutamylcysteine synthatase; GPx, glutathione peroxidase; GST, glutathione
  S-transferase; HO-1, heme oxygenase-1; Keap 1, Kelch-like ECH-associated protein
  1; Nrf2, Nuclear factor E2-related factor 2; PRX, peroxiredoxin; SOD, superoxide
  dismutase; Trx, thioredoxin.
