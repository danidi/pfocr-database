caption: A network of upstream signaling pathways comprised of the oncogenes and tumor
  suppressors listed control the activation status of mTORC1 by regulating the TSC1-TSC2
  complex. Oncogenic signaling events inhibit the TSC1-TSC2 complex to promote the
  accumulation of Rheb-GTP and subsequent activation of mTORC1. Reciprocally, through
  their inhibitory effects on oncogenic signaling, tumor suppressors in effect stimulate
  the TSC1-TSC2 complex to inhibit the activation of mTORC1 by Rheb. In addition to
  its previously defined roles in promoting protein synthesis and blocking autophagy,
  mTORC1 activation can drive specific metabolic processes through regulation of metabolic
  gene expression. These include glucose uptake and glycolysis through HIF1α and the
  pentose phosphate pathway and lipid biosynthesis through SREBP (see text for details).
