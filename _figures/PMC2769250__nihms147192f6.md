caption: C. elegans lacks the entire heme biosynthetic pathway and acquires heme in
  toto for incorporation into hemoproteins. Worms absorb heme as a nutrient in the
  gut via the apical intestinal surface. The heme is either transported directly via
  membrane-bound transporters (HRG-1) and hemochaperones or may be transported via
  intracellular vesicles. Worms must also have an intercellular heme transport system
  to mobilize heme from the twenty intestinal cells to other cell types including
  neurons, muscles, and developing embryos.
