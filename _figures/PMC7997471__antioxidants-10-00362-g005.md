  effects. IMP could interfere with the LPS binding to LBP, CD14, and MD-2 on the

  and the results in this manuscript, IMP attenuates the inflammatory effect cascade
  via inhibition of transcription factors including IκB/NF-κB, MAPK/AP-1, and JAKs/STATs
  inflammatory transcription factor signaling pathways. Moreover, IMP activates the
  antioxidative transcription factor Nrf2 signaling pathway in LPS-induced RAW264.7
  murine macrophage cells.
