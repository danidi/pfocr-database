caption: In inflammatory macrophages, glycogen is synthesized and then channeled through
  glycogenolysis to generate G6P and further through the pentose phosphate pathway
  to yield abundant NADPH, ensuring high levels of reduced glutathione for inflammatory
  macrophage survival. Glycogen metabolism also increases UDPG levels and the receptor
  P2Y14 in macrophages. The UDPG/P2Y14signaling pathway not only upregulates the expression
  of STAT1 via activating RARβ but also promotes STAT1 phosphorylation by downregulating
  phosphatase TC45.
