caption: GC-C activation, intracellular effects. (A) The GC-C receptor is activated
  by both endogenous and exogenous ligands, which increase intracellular cGMP levels.
  The subsequent regulation of protein kinases, phosphodiesterases and ion channels
  modulates a number of downstream physiological mechanisms including fluid and electrolyte
  secretion, barrier function, inflammation and proliferation. (B) Dysregulation of
  this signalling pathway in turn produces epithelial dysfunction, which may contribute
  to chronic constipation, IBDs and intestinal tumourigenesis. CFTR, cystic fibrosis
  transmembrane conductance regulator; GC-C, guanylate cyclase-C; GTP, guanosine triphosphate;
  MLCK, myosin light chain kinase; PDE3, phosphodiesterase 3; NHE3, Na+/H+ exchanger-3;
  PKA, protein kinase A; PKG II, protein kinase G II; TJP, tight junction protein;
  TNFα, tumour necrosis factor alpha.
