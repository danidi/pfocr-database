caption: Copper, taken up via CTR1 (red) is transferred to chaperones ATOX1, CCS and
  COX17, which ferry it (black dash arrows) to ATP7A/B (blue) in the Golgi, to Cu–Zn
  superoxide dismutase (magenta) in the cytosol and to cytochrome c oxidase (green)
  in the mitochondria, respectively. In the Golgi, ATP7A/B load Cu on newly synthetized
  cuproenzymes (orange ball), which traffic along the biosynthetic pathway (orange
  arrow). Significant increase in intracellular Cu induces export of ATP7A/B (blue
  arrow) towards post-Golgi compartments and plasma membrane where ATP7A/B drive the
  efflux of excessive Cu from the cell. Solid black arrows indicate Cu translocation
  across the membrane.
