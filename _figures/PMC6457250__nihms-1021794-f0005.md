caption: 'Glucose metabolism and mitochondrial activity regulate macrophage function
  through two major pathways: (1) Excess glucose uptake increases pyruvate and heats
  up the mitochondrial electron transport chain; resulting in ROS production. Mitochondrial
  ROS force PKM2 into dimeric formation and PKM2 dimers are translocated into the
  nucleus, where they phosphorylate STAT3. This triggers production of IL-1β and IL-6.
  (2) Pyruvate import into the mitochondrion induces production of BMP4, which activates
  SMAD1/5 phosphorylation and upregulation of the transcription factor IRF1. IRF1
  amplifies transcription of PD-L1, enabling the cell to deliver immunoinhibitory
  signals.'
