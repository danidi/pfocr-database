caption: Four androgen metabolism pathways to DHT synthesis. Pathway 1 is the 5α-reduction
  of T to DHT (dashed lines). Pathway 2 uses adrenal androgens, DHEA or ASD, to synthesize
  T or 5α-dione that are converted to DHT (faint gray long dashes). Pathway 3 is the
  cholesterol pathway (small gray dashes). Pathway 4 is the backdoor pathway of DHT
  synthesis using androstanediol instead of T to generate DHT (outlined in bold).
  Abiraterone inhibits 17α-hydroxylase and 17,20-lyase of CYP17A1 (relevant to Pathways
  2 and 4) whereas TAK-700 or TOK-001 inhibit only 17,20-lyase (relevant to Pathway
  2).
