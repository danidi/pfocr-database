caption: Testicular and ovotesticular differentiation in XX foetuses. In individuals
  with Yp translocations, the presence of SRY triggers testicular differentiation
  (SRY-positive cases). In SRY-negative individuals, testicular development may result
  from overexpression of “pro-testicular” factors (e.g., SOX9, SOX3, SOX10, FGF9,
  DMRT1) due to duplications/triplications of gene coding or regulatory sequences,
  or from inactivating mutations of “pro-ovarian” factors (e.g., RSPO1, WNT4, FOXL2)
  or factors that are believed to favour the gene dosage balance through the ovarian
  pathway (WT1, SF1, COUP-TF2). Generally, though not always, testicular DSD presents
  with male genitalia and ovotesticular DSD with ambiguous genitalia.
