caption: Renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system. Renin secretion is the first step in
  the activation of the RAAS pathway. Renin cleaves angiotensinogen to form Ang I,
  which is then transformed into Ang II by ACE and chymase enzyme. Specific receptors,
  AT1R and AT2R, can then bind Ang II. AT1R stimulates blood pressure, cardiac remodeling,
  and atherosclerosis, whereas AT2R has opposite effects. ACE2 cleaves Ang I and Ang
  II to form Ang(1–9) to Ang(1–7), respectively. Ang(1–7) induces vasodilation, anti-inflammatory,
  antifibrotic, and anti-remodeling effects, through MasR and AT2R. ACE, angiotensin-converting
  enzyme; ACE2, angiotensin-converting enzyme 2; Ang, angiotensin; AT1R, angiotensin
  II type 1 receptor; AT2R, angiotensin II type 2 receptor; MasR, Mas receptor.
