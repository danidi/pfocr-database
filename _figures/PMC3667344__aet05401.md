caption: Proposed mechanisms of anaesthetic-induced neurodegeneration in the newborn
  involving the intrinsic and extrinsic apoptotic cell death pathways. Suppression
  of synaptic signalling is proposed to attenuate neurotrophic survival signals leading
  to activation of the intrinsic apoptotic cascade. This involves mobilization of
  the pro-apopotic protein Bax, which in turn induces cytochrome c (Cyto. C) release
  from mitochondria, activation of caspase-9, and subsequent induction of caspase
  3 cleavage to provoke apoptosis. Anti-apoptotic effectors such as Bcl oppose this
  pathway. The extrinsic pathway is stimulated by ligands binding to death receptors
  located on the cell membrane that cause caspase 8 activation, which then cleaves
  and activates caspase 3. These ligands include cytokines such as TNF-α.
