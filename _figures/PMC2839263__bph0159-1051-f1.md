caption: Schematic summary of possible localization of β-arrestin related to wingless
  (from Drosophila) and the oncogene int-1 (WNT) signalling in cells. In unstimulated
  cells, β-arrestin is evenly distributed in the cytosol. When Dishevelled (DVL) is
  present, it co-localizes with non-membraneous DVL aggregates (A). Upon phosphorylation
  of DVL with casein kinase-1ε (CK1ε), β-arrestin becomes cytosolic again (B). In
  the presence of Frizzled (FZD), receptor tyrosine kinase RYK (RYK) and/or PAR1 β-arrestins
  are recruited to the plasma membrane (submembranous) (C) for further association
  with clathrin-coated pits upon ligand binding (D). Subsequently, the ligand-receptor-β-arrestin
  complex can be internalized through the endosomal pathway (E). The insert shows
  a mouse-embryonic fibroblast co-expressing β-arrestin-2 (green) and DVL-2 (red).
