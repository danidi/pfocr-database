caption: (A) In WT PEMs, SPI-2-compentent Salmonella target FAK-Akt signaling for
  activation and recruitment to the SCV membrane. The increase in FAK and Akt activity
  is paralleled by a robust increase in the activation of mTORC1, which suppresses
  the autophagic response and promotes bacterial survival. Phamacological inhibition
  of FAK (PF-228) or Akt (AKTV/triciribine) inhibited LC3 recruitment to ΔinvG SCVs,
  whereas inhibition of mTORC1 (rapamycin) enhanced LC3 colocalization with bacteria,
  phenocopying conditions observed in FAK-deficient macrophages. (B) In the absence
  of FAK, Akt remains inactive and is unable to activate mTORC1. LC3 is more efficiently
  recruited to SCVs resulting in autophagosome formation and the enhanced elimination
  of intracellular bacteria. Ly, lysosome.
