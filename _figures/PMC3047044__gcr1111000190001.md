caption: Simplified schematic version of the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR)
  signaling cascade. Stimulation of EGFR by ligands such as epidermal growth factor
  (EGF) or transforming growth factor-α (TGF-α) results in dimerization and phosphorylation
  of the receptor. This triggers activation of K-ras through the small adaptor proteins
  Grb2 and Sos. K-ras functions as the central distributor of the signal via both
  phospho-inositol kinases (P3K) and BRAF. PI3K inhibits apoptosis through AKT activation,
  while BRAF stimulates cell growth and proliferation through the mitogen-activated
  protein kinase (MAPK) pathway. The BRAF kinase is downstream of K-ras, and this
  explains why wild-type status of both K-ras and BRAF are necessary for EGFR blockade
  to work.
