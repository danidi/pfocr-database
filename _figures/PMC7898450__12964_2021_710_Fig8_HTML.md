caption: L-plastin Ser5 phosphorylation cascade in cancer cells. The ERK/MAPK and
  PI3K pathways are frequently dysregulated in cancer. Upon activation of these signalling
  pathways, their downstream effector kinases RSK1/2 and SGK3, respectively, are able
  to phosphorylate L-plastin on its residue Ser5. This phosphorylation leads to increased
  L-plastin bundling activity as well as enhanced recruitment to invadopodia and ECM
  degradation, promoting the invasiveness of the cancer cell
