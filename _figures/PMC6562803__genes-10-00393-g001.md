caption: The phosphoinositide metabolic pathway in plants. The free hydroxyl groups
  of the phosphatidylinositols (PtdIns) can be phosphorylated at positions 3, 4, and/or
  5 by distinct kinases (black arrows) to generate various phosphoinositides. Phospholipase
  C (PLC) can convert substrate PtdIns(4,5)P2 into the second messenger Ins(1,4,5)P3.
  The action of inositol polyphosphate 5-phosphatases (red arrows) modulates the signaling
  functions of these molecules.
