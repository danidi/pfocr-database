caption: Proposed action mechanism of 6SL in TP-induced BPH rats and BPH-1 cells.
  6SL demonstrated anti-proliferative effects in a testosterone-induced BPH rat model
  and on BPH-1 cells by regulating the pRB/E2F1–AR pathway. 6SL inhibited prostate
  enlargement via controlling androgen/AR signaling-dependent hyperproliferation.
  AR influences E2F-regulated transcription that plays a paramount role in cell cycle
  progression and apoptosis signaling. Here, inhibition of E2F1/pRb and cell cycle
  progression by 6SL may be attributed to its anti-proliferative properties, supporting
  its regulation of the pRB/E2F1–AR network.
