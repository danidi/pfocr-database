caption: 'Apical signals inhibit nuclear YAP localisation. A: In columnar epithelia,
  YAP/TAZ is cytoplasmic in differentiated cells with an apical domain and nuclear
  in basal layer stem cells which lack an apical domain and contact the basal lamina
  ECM via Integrins. B: Crumbs‐Merlin‐Kibra‐Salvador‐MST‐LATS signalling (the canonical
  Hippo pathway) leads to phosphorylation of YAP/TAZ and retention in the cytoplasm
  (due to binding to 14‐3‐3 proteins) despite contact with the ECM. Thus, strong apical
  Hippo signalling is able to overcome basal Integrin signalling to maintain YAP/TAZ
  in the cytoplasm.'
