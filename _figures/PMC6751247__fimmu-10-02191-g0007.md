caption: A schematic description of TLR7-mediated enhancement of CD8+ T cell function.
  TLR7 stimulation promotes the effector functions of CD8+ T cells if CD8+ T cells
  are primed by αCD3 and express TLR7. TLR7-mediated T cell activation is dependent
  on MyD88 and AKT-mTOR signaling, leading to the expression of immune-related changes
  and metabolic alterations in CD8+ T cells, including the upregulation of glucose
  uptake and glycolysis. Glycolysis was found to be regulated by the AKT-mTOR pathway
  and a downstream transcription factor IRF4. Glycolysis is critical for TLR7-mediated
  CD8+ T cell activation and enhanced effector functions of CD8+ T cells. Solid arrows
  represent signaling pathways identified in this study. Broken arrows indicate potential
  signaling pathways.
