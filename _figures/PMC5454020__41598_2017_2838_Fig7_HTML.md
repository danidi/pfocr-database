caption: GLP-1R signalling promotes β-cell glucose metabolism via mTOR-dependent HIF-1α
  activation. Upon GLP-1 binding, the G-protein coupled GLP-1R receptor activates
  adenylyl cyclase (AC), increasing intracellular levels of cAMP. Signalling mediated
  by cAMP promotes the mTOR pathway in β-cells, which, in turn, induces translational
  activation of HIF-1α. In the nucleus, HIF-1α drives transcriptional up-regulation
  of glycolytic genes. Increased pool of glycolytic enzymes in the cytosol allows
  for an elevated glycolytic flux and glycolytic capacity, resulting in faster generation
  of ATP and enhanced glucose stimulated insulin secretion (GSIS).
