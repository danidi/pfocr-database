caption: Hsps can suppress p53 and allow proliferation of cells with activated oncogenes.
  Oncogenes of Ras pathway activate two parallel pathways, including the proliferation
  response and the growth inhibitory p53 pathway. Normally p53 induces p21 and causes
  growth arrest and senescence, thus preventing cell proliferation. However, proliferating
  cells acquire mutations in the p53 pathway. Alternatively, high levels of Hsps inhibit
  p53 and allow cells to proliferate.
