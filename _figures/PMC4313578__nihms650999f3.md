caption: Hormones (insulin) and growth factors (IGF-1) signal through the PI3K/AKT
  pathway. AKT phosphorylates TSC2 and inhibits the TSC1/2 complex, leading to conversion
  of inactive RHEB-GDP to active RHEB-GTP and subsequent activation of mTORC1. Abundance
  of amino acids activate mTORC1 through RAG GTPases. AKT also activates mTORC1 by
  phosphorylating PRAS40, which is a negative regulator of mTORC1. AMPK is activated
  in response to low ATP levels and inactivates mTORC1 by phosphorylating TSC2 and
  RAPTOR. Resveratrol, metformin, and statins activate AMPK. Rapamycin inhibits mTOR
  activity.
