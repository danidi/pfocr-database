caption: MUC1-C forms complexes with EGFR at the cell membrane that are mediated by
  extracellular galectin-3 bridges. Stimulation of EGFR with EGF induces phosphorylation
  of the MUC1-C cytoplasmic domain, promotes binding of the PI3K SH2 domains and thereby
  activation of the PI3K->AKT->mTOR pathway. mTOR-mediated phosphorylation and activation
  of S6K1 induces degradation of PDCD4, an inhibitor of the eIF4A RNA helicase. Derepression
  of eIF4A activity stimulates MUC1-C translation with marked increases in MUC1-C
  protein and, in turn, the formation of EGFR/MUC1-C complexes. This autoinductive
  loop is constitutively activated in breast cancer cells and disrupted by the eIF4A
  inhibitors, silvestrol and CR-1-31-B.
