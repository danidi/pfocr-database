caption: Schematic illustration of the signaling pathway involved in MT-mediated GCs
  resistance to hypoxia-induced apoptosis. Hypoxia facilitates ROS accumulation and
  the cleavage of the initiator caspases (8, 9), which in turn activates the executioner
  caspase 3, thereby inducing GCs apoptosis. In the presence of MT, the apoptosis
  of GCs is inhibited, possibly through the clearance of cellular ROS, activation
  of antioxidant enzymes expression, or suppression of Caspase 8/9-Caspase 3 axis
  via the MT–MTNR1B–PKA pathway.
