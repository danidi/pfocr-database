caption: Bile acids (CA and CDCA), synthetic FXR agonists (GW4064, INT-747 and WAY-362450)
  and a dual FXR/TGR5 agonist (INT-767) activate hepatic FXR, resulting in modulation
  of hepatic and plasma lipid homeostasis. FXR lowers hepatic triglyceride levels
  likely through multiple mechanisms. The FXR-SHP-SREBP-1c pathway does not appear
  to play a role in this process [, –]. Instead, other genes/pathways, including FGF21
  [], ChREBP [], PPARα [, ], and AKR1B7 [] may play a role in FXR-mediated reduction
  in hepatic triglyceride levels. Activation of FXR regulates plasma cholesterol and
  triglyceride levels through modulating several genes, including SR-BI [], LDLR [],
  Syndecan-1[], VLDLR [], ApoCII, ApoCIII [] and ANGPTL3 [].
