caption: Wnt signaling at vertebrate neuromuscular junctions. (A) Mouse Wnt3 and zebrafish
  Wnt11r function during prepatterning. Wnt11r signaling through Unplugged/MuSK before
  innervation leads to aneural AChR clustering and also via a parallel signaling pathway
  to changes in extracellular matrix modifications (EMMs), possibly via chondroitin
  sulfate proteoglycans (CSPGs) that eventually influence incoming motor neuron axonal
  growth cone guidance. Wnt3 signaling leads to the rapid and transient activation
  of Rac1 and AChR microcluster formation, which (B) in the presence of neural agrin
  at the time of innervation are stabilized in exact opposition to the presynaptic
  terminal.
