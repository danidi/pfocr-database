caption: Diagram of the transactivation assay used to detect the intracellular liberation
  of Zn2+ in neurons. Cd2+ enters cells via a non-specified pathway or through activated
  N-methyl-D-aspartate (NMDA) receptors. Once inside, Cd2+ can displace bound Zn2+
  from metallothionein (MT). The liberated Zn2+, in turn, binds to metal response
  element (MRE)-binding transcription factor-1 (MTF-1). MTF-1 then translocates into
  the nucleus and transactivates an MRE-driven luciferase reporter gene.
