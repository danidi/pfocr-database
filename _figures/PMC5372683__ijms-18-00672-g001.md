caption: Modulators and signaling pathway of group I mGluRs. Group I mGluRs can be
  activated by orthosteric modulators independently or be triggered by positive allosteric
  modulators in the absence of agonists, for example l-Glu or Ca2+. Activation of
  group I mGluRs recruits G-proteins, thereby activating PLC which subsequently decomposes
  PIP2 into DAG and IP3. IP3 then opens inositol trisphosphate receptor (IP3R) on
  the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane to release Ca2+ into cytosol, thus opening
  the Ca2+ channel on cell membrane. At the same time, group I mGluRs also can couple
  to the cAMP pathway by activating AC which quickly converts ATP to cAMP, thereby
  activating protein kinase A (PKA).
