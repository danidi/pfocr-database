caption: Six important signaling networks of osteoblast differentiation. Binding of
  Wnt to the FZD receptor induces ß-catenin accumulation, which translocates to the
  nucleus to activate target gene transcription. Several transcription factors have
  been found crucial for osteoblast differentiation downstream of this signaling pathway,
  such as Runx2, Osterix, and ATF4. They are essential for differentiation of mesenchymal
  stem cells into differentiated osteoblasts and also function in the transcription
  of osteoclast-specific genes. The dotted lines indicate that the physiological function
  or the stage at which the factor mainly works remains to be proven.
