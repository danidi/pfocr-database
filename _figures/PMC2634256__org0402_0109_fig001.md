caption: Three Wnt signaling pathways (modified from Huelsken and Behrens, 2002).
  (A) The Wnt/β-catenin pathway. Wnt signal is transduced through the frizzled receptor
  (Fz) via dishevelled (Dsh), which represses the axin/glycogen synthase kinase-3β
  (GSK3β) complex, which induces the degradation of β-catenin. Accumulated Cytoplasmic
  β-catenin is translocated to the nucleus where it binds with Tcf/Lef, thus activating
  the transcription of its target gene. (B) The planar cell polarity (PCP) pathway
  activated in response to Wnt signaling signals via the small GTPases Rho and Cdc42
  to c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK), acting primarily on the cytoskeleton (C) An alternative
  pathway stimulates the release of intracellular Ca2+, activating protein kinase
  C (PKC) and Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent kinase II (CamKII).
