caption: The insulin/insulin growth factor signaling pathway and its activation by
  reactive oxygen species (ROS). Insulin or other growth factors bind to and activate
  the insulin receptor or other receptor tyrosine kinases. This binding leads to PI3K
  and Akt activation either directly or via Ras. Akt phosphorylates and inhibits the
  activities of Foxo and the Tor inhibitor Tsc1/Tsc2. Activated Tor impairs autophagy
  and activates protein synthesis, whereas activated Foxo has the opposite effects.
  Phosphatases inhibit signaling either by catalyzing receptor dephosphorylation or
  by antagonizing PI3K activity. Pathway activators are shown in blue, and inhibitors
  in gray. ROS inhibits pathway inhibitors (red) and activates pathway activators
  (green).
