caption: Proposed model of pleiotropic roles of Ca+2/calmodulin-dependent (CAMK) pathways
  regulating cadmium toxicity in human osteoblast-like cells. The calmodulin-dependent
  PDE pathway facilitates cadmium-induced ERK activation leading to apoptosis, while
  having no detectable effect cadmium-induced ALP activity. In contrast, the CAMKK
  pathway plays a protective role against cadmium-induced osteotoxicity via ERK signaling.
  This study did not detect involvement of the CAMKII pathway in cadmium-induced osteotoxicity.
