caption: Differential signaling pathway by membrane-bound FasL (mFasL) and soluble
  FasL (sFasL). mFasL leads to the recruitment of FADD and activates caspase pathways.
  mFas promotes apoptosis. mFasL is shed by a disintegrin and metalloproteinases (ADAM)
  or MMPs in the pathological or physiological condition. For instance, sFasL strongly
  activates extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK), PI3K/Akt, and c-jun N-terminal
  kinase (JNK) to induce proliferation and inhibit apoptotic activity of the fibroblast-like
  synoviocytes. Differential signal by the engagement of sFasL is determined by the
  cell type and the pathologic condition.
