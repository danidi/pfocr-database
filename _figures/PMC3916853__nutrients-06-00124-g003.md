caption: Schematic illustration of lycopene metabolic pathway by β carotene-9′,10′-oxygenase
  (BCO2). 5-(Z) and 13-(Z) lycopene are preferentially cleaved by BCO2 at 9′10′-double
  bond. The cleavage product, apo-10′-lycopenal, can be further oxidized to apo-10′-lycopenol
  or reduced to apo-10′-lycopenoic acid (APO10LA), dependent on the presence of NADH.
  Whether apolycopenoids can be oxidized to form acyclic retinoids remains to be elucidated.
