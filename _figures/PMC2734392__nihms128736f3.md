caption: NAD biosynthetic pathways in yeast, invertebrates, and mammals. (A) The NAD
  biosynthetic pathways in the budding yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae and invertebrates,
  such as C. elegans and Drosophila. PNC1, NPT1, NMA1 and NMA2, QNS1, and QPT1 are
  nicotinamidase, nicotinic acid phosphoribosyltransferase, nicotinic acid mononucleotide
  adenylyltransferase 1 and 2, NAD synthetase, and quinolinic acid phosphoribosyltransfease,
  respectively. Only SIR2 is shown as a representative NAD-dependent protein deacetylase.
  NIC, nicotinamide; NA, nicotinic acid; NaMN, nicotinic acid mononucleotide. B) The
  NAD biosynthetic pathways in mammals. The de novo pathway and the NAD biosynthetic
  pathway from nicotinic acid are evolutionarily conserved, while the NAD biosynthetic
  pathway from nicotinamide (red arrows) is vertebrate-specific and mediated by nicotinamide
  phosphoribosyltransferase (NAMPT). Nicotinamide is the main precursor for NAD biosynthesis
  in mammals. While multiple enzymes break NAD into nicotinamide and ADP-ribose, only
  SIRT1 is shown here. NMN, nicotinamide mononucleotide. C) The reaction catalyzed
  by NAMPT. PPi, inorganic pyrophosphate.
