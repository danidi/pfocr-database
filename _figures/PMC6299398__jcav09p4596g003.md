caption: The consequences of driver gene mutations in canonical Wnt signaling. Canonical
  Wnt activation results in CTTNB1 accumulation by inhibiting the destruction complex
  through phosphorylation of GSK3β. Activation of Akt from the PIK3CA/Akt pathway
  also enhances the phosphorylation of GSK3β, which in turn inhibits the phosphorylation
  and proteosomal degradation of CTNNB1. This permits CTNNB1 to enter the nucleus
  and activate transcription of Wnt responsive genes. FBXW7 loss-of-function mutations
  may enhance Wnt activation through CTNNB1 accumulation.
