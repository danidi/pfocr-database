caption: 'Schematic diagram of the functional role of skeletal muscle-derived neurotrophic
  factors after exercise. Exercise (neuromuscular activity) increases BDNF expression
  in skeletal muscle. In the patients with spinal cord injury, BDNF stimulates protein
  synthesis by activating Akt/mTOR/p70S6K pathway through TrkB receptor on muscle
  membrane. BDNF also promotes the fat oxidation through AMPK-ACC signaling. BDNF
  produced by skeletal muscle after exercise may circulate into brain to improve the
  impaired learning and/or depression. Increased GDNF protein after exercise promotes
  the amount of neurotransmitter (e.g., ACh) at NMJ by conjugating with Ret in presynaptic
  region (axon terminal). NT-4/5 may possess similar role of GDNF. BDNF: brain-derived
  neurotrophic factor, GDNF: glial cell-line derived neurotrophic factor, NT-4/5:
  neurotrophin-4/5, NMJ: neuromuscular junction, TORC1: a component of TOR signaling
  complex 1, Rheb: Ras homolog enriched in brain, mTOR: mammalian target of rapamycin,
  AMPK: AMP-activated protein kinase, and ACC: acetyl CoA carboxylase.'
