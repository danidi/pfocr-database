caption: Activin signaling regulates transcription of the FSHβ gene. Activin signaling,
  via ActRIIA/B and ALK4/7 receptors, results in the phosphorylation of Smad2 and
  3, which then dimerize with the common Smad4 and translocate into the nucleus of
  the cell. Activated Smad3, and potentially Smad2, regulate transcription of FSHβ
  and other specific target genes. The inhibitory Smad7 prevents the phosphorylation
  of Smad3. Activin signaling also results in activation of the p38 MAPK signaling
  pathway, potentially via TAK1, however, it is still unclear how p38 relays activin
  signaling to the FSHβ promoter.
