caption: Proposed model for the effect of HPTE in the granulosa cell. FSH binding
  to the FSH receptor initiates the PKA signaling pathway that promotes E2 production
  in granulosa cells. HPTE appears to inhibit this cascade through an unknown mechanism,
  but effects in multiple pathways are implicated. HPTE affected the expression of
  several genes known to control granulosa cell function, as described in the text.
  Among these, activin, TGFβs, GREM1, SF1, IGF-1, IGFBPs, and caspases all have distinct
  effects in controlling granulosa cell growth, differentiation, survival, and steroidogenesis.
  White arrows indicate induction. Gray arrows indicate inhibition. Gsα, α subunit
  of stimulatory guanine nucleotide–binding protein; CREB, cAMP-response element–binding
  protein; SF-1, steroidogenic factor 1; ▵4, androstenedione; E1, estrone; PI-3K,
  phosphoinositide 3-kinase; TFGβ, transforming growth factor-β; GSK3, glycogen synthase
  kinase-3.
