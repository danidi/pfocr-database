caption: The mTOR pathway responds to growth factors via the class I PI3K pathway.
  Inductive signals inhibit the mTOR kinase, which stimulates a partial dephosphorylation
  of Atg13. This allows the assembly of Atg13 with Atg 1 and Atg 17 (Atg-1 kinase
  complex) and the activity of the complex activates the autophagy cascade. Atg12
  and LC3 are ubiquitin-like proteins; during autophagosome formation Atg12 is conjugated
  to Atg5 and LC3 binds phosphatidylethanolamine (PE), Atg7 and Atg3 to form LC3-II.
  Atg16 binds a pair of Atg12-Atg5 conjugates and this complex participates in autophagosomal
  membrane formation. The (Atg12-Atg5)2-Atg16 complex is required for the conjugation
  of LC3-II in the autophagosomal membrane. After completion of autophagosome formation
  LC3-II is reverted in LC3-I, which is released from the membrane. Proposed cytokine
  regulation checkpoints within this pathway are illustrated.
