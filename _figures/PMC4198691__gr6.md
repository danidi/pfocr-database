caption: PDLIM2 has an essential function in maintaining breast epithelial cell polarity
  at the level of feedback signaling through the β1-integrin–FAK–RhoA axis. Suppression
  of PDLIM2 expression prevents negative feedback control of FAK-ROCK-RhoA signaling
  downstream of β1-integrin resulting in hyperactivation of the pathway causing dysregulation
  of polarization, proliferation/apoptosis, cell-cell and cell-matrix adhesion, and
  loss of normal phenotype of breast epithelial cells.
