caption: Signaling pathways important for stem/progenitor cells regulation. (A) Notch
  signaling pathway. Cell-cell interaction is required for lateral inhibition. The
  sending cell produces Delta which binds and activates Notch in the adjacent cell.
  Matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) such as Adam10 and Adam17 proteolytically cleave
  Notch extracellular domain while gamma secretase cleaves intracellular domain, leading
  to transcriptional activation of Notch target genes which feedback to inhibit Notch
  in the sending cell. (B) Wnt signaling pathway. In the absence of ligand (left),
  β-catenin is inhibited by the destruction complex. Binding of Wnt ligand to receptors
  frees β-catenin which displaces the transcriptional repressor Groucho. Together
  with the T cell factor (TCF) transcription factor, β-catenin activates Wnt target
  genes including some of its own receptors. Of note, R-spondin can potentiate Wnt
  signaling through inhibiting ZNRF3/RNF43 which normally inhibits the Wnt transmembrane
  receptor Frizzled. DKK, Dickkopf.
