caption: 'Low-dose IL-2 selectively activates high-affinity IL2R-expressing Tregs,
  but has limited effect on Teffs. IL2R signaling relies mostly on the Jak3/STAT5
  pathway in Tregs. In contrast, the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway is highly dispensable for
  the generation, expansion and survival of Tregs, yet not of Teff. Hence, mTORi favors
  Tregs over Teffs. Similarly, the critical role of anti-apoptotic Bcl2/Bcl-xL genes
  in hindering mitochondrial apoptosis in Teffs, but not Tregs, makes the former more
  vulnerable to Bcl2/Bcl-xL inhibition. Exposure of FOXP3+ Treg cells to pan-HDACi
  promotes the stability and function of Tregs, through acetylation of the FOXP3 gene.
  Tregs are unaffected by anti-CD40 antibody as they do not upregulate CD40L expression.Abbreviations:
  HA IL2R, high affinity IL-2 receptor; HDAC, histone deacetylase; HDACi, histone
  deacetylase inhibitor; LA IL2R, low affinity IL-2 receptor; mTORi, mammalian target
  of rapamycin inhibitor; NFAT, nuclear factor of activated T-cells; PD-1, programmed
  cell death protein 1; PTEN, phosphatase and tensin homolog; TCR, T cell receptor;
  Teff, effector T cells; Treg, regulatory T cell; TSDR, Treg-specific demethylated
  region.'
