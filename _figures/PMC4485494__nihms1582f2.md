caption: The binding of insulin to its cell surface receptor triggers the recruitment
  and activation of PI3K. At the plasma membrane, PI3K stimulates the formation of
  phosphatidylinositol-3,4,5trisphosphate (PtdIns (3,4,5)P3) which triggers the colocalisation
  of phosphoinositide dependent kinase 1 (PDK1) and PKB allowing PDK1 to activate
  PKB. Upon activation, PKB phosphorylates and inactivates GSK-3 resulting in the
  dephosphorylation of glycogen synthase and eIF2B, two substrates of GSK-3 that control
  the rates of glycogen metabolism and protein synthesis respectively.
