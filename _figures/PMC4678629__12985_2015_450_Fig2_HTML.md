caption: Classical pathways of type I IFN induction and HCV mediating IFN resistance.
  HCV dsRNA is detected by the retinoic acid inducible gene I (RIG-I) and the toll-like
  receptor 3 (TLR3) and subsequently triggers the cascade of adaptor proteins, mitochondrial
  antiviral signaling protein (MAVS) and TIR-domain containing adapter-inducing interferon-β
  (TRIF), respectively. This leads to the activation of the type I IFN induction pathway.
  Binding of IFNs to their cellular receptors activates an intracellular signaling
  cascade via the Jak/STAT signaling pathway and leads to the up-regulation of a number
  of interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs) expression [–]
