caption: The two predominant DSB repair pathways in mammalian cells. (A) Schematic
  of c-NHEJ repair of blunt or minimally processed DNA ends. This pathway is active
  throughout the cell cycle and the result is accurate repair or small insertions
  or deletions (indels). (B) Schematic of HDR of postreplicative (S/G2) DSBs. This
  pathway requires the generation of 3′ overhangs competent for loading Rad51. Single-strand
  annealing (SSA), resulting in deletions, can also occur if excessive resection exposes
  regions of homology. Accurate repair is achieved either by SDSA or HR followed by
  dissolution or resolution of the dHJ.
