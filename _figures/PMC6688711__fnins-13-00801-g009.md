caption: Schematic of a proposed model of tau hyperphosphorylation under the conditions
  of normal and impaired insulin signaling. (A) Insulin signaling in a physiological
  environment activates AKT, which in turn inactivates GSK-3β and prevents tau hyperphosphorylation.
  Insulin also activates TOR pathway for cellular growth and maintains a basal level
  of autophagy for cell survival. (B) An impaired insulin signaling results in an
  inactivation of AKT and an activation of GSK-3βY216 resulting in tau hyperphosphorylation
  leading to the formation of insoluble tau aggregates. The pathological condition
  is exacerbated by an impaired clearance of toxic protein.
