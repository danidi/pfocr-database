caption: REDD1 controls stress-induced reduction in O2 consumption and anabolic processes.
  In the basal state, activation of the Akt/mTOR pathway promotes (i) anabolic processes
  (protein and glycogen synthesis), (ii) the mitochondria/ER interaction, and (iii)
  oxygen-dependent ATP production. In response to energetic stress, REDD1 inhibits
  the Akt/mTOR pathway and interacts with MAMs, leading to a coordinated decrease
  in MAMs extent, mitochondrial oxygen consumption, and anabolic processes. This mechanism
  could contribute to spare ATP in stressful conditions
