  of autophagy, while ROS production is rendered by curcumin, resulting in activation
  of apoptotic signaling []. Taken together, curcumin exerts inhibition of ovarian
  cancer cell proliferation. Curcumin reduces the stemness of ovarian cancer cells
  through downregulation of Wnt/β-catenin [], Sonic Hedgehog [] and Notch pathways
  []. Curcumin inhibits the adhesion and invasion of ovarian cancer cells to peritoneal
  mesothelial lines via inhibition of NFκβ signaling activation []. Akt (pathway):
  a serine/threonine kinase involving cell survival and growth; ROS/RNS: Reactive
  Oxygen Species and Reactive Nitrogen Species; Wnt/β-catenin: a group of signal transduction
  pathways associated with tumour development; NFκβ: nuclear factor κ beta.'
