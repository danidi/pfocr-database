caption: Potential intracellular target points for (i) acute alcohol and (ii) chronic
  alcohol in a stylized cell. The induction of canonical NF-κB with p50–p65 translocation
  to nucleus via pattern recognition receptors (PRR) is outlined by, for example,
  TLR4 and MyD88 activation. The non-canonical NF-κB pathway with p52-RelB is detailed
  with CD40 as the respective receptor. Either pathway leads to the transcription
  of inflammatory cytokines (e.g., TNF-α or important immune regulatory proteins potentiating,
  for example, inflammasome formation.) Inflammasome formation itself is comprised
  of ASC, Caspase-1, and NLRP3 and regulated via ionic currents or intracellular PRRs,
  like nucleotide-binding oligomerization domain (NOD). Another cell compartment under
  alcohol influence is the phagosome needed for ingestion and destruction of pathogens
  using an array of reactive oxygen species.
