caption: c-di-GMP metabolic pathway. Diguanylate cyclases (DGCs) with a GGDEF domain
  synthesize c-di-GMP, which is degraded by phosphodiesterases (PDEs) with an HD-GYP
  or an EAL domain. Phosphodiesterases degrade c-di-GMP to pGpG first then GMP. Phosphodiesterases
  with the EAL domain degrade pGpG to GMP slower than those with the HD-GYP domain.
  Generally, c-di-GMP promotes bacterial biofilm formation. When DGC enzyme activity
  is lowered by chemical inhibitors, less amount of c-di-GMP is produced, then biofilm
  formation by bacteria is discouraged.
