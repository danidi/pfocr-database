caption: Modular Schema of the Stress Response Network. Integration of findings from
  the experimental Energy Restriction in Quiescence (ERiQ) model with knowledge of
  stress response signaling constitutes a comprehensive network. Autoregulatory loops
  (elliptic connectors) provide model stability and reject sudden perturbations. Substantial
  changes in the energetic state of the cell, such as a slow buildup of mitochondrial
  dysfunction, alter the activity of key signaling nodes involving energy stress sensors
  AMPK, PTEN and Sirtuins, responding to alterations in ATP, NADP and NAD+, respectively
  (dashed lines). The overall effect is a pro-survival phenotype comprising activation
  of the AKT pathway and transcription factor NF-κB, suppression of p53, and compensation
  of ATP production using glycolysis (dotted lines).
