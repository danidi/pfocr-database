caption: Metabolic pathway of ω-6 fatty acids. Linoleic acid (LA) comes from diet,
  and through ∆-6 desaturase enzyme forms gamma-linoleic acid (GLA). Then, an elongase
  (CE) adds two carbon atoms to form dihomo-γ-linolenic acid (DGLA) that, with the
  action of ∆-5 desaturase, produces arachidonic acid (AA). AA through cyclooxygenase
  (COX) enzymes is the precursor of some prostaglandins (PG), as the H2, I2, E2, D2,
  F2 and the thromboxane (TX) A2 involved in different processes associated with cancer.
