caption: The cyclin D/cyclin-dependent kinase (CDK)4/6/retinoblastoma (Rb) Pathway
  and the cell cycle. The mammalian cell cycle is tightly regulated. In the context
  of breast cancer, both steroid and peptide growth factors drive proliferation through
  cyclin D/CDK4/6 activation. This results in the hyper-phosporylation of pRb as G1
  progresses. When retinoblastoma protein (pRb) is hyper-phosphorylated, the transcription
  factor E2F is released and the cell cycle progresses through S phase. Small molecule
  kinase inhibitors of CDK4/6 aim to block the hyper-phosphorylation of pRb inducing
  a G1 arrest and preventing proliferation. ER estrogen receptor
