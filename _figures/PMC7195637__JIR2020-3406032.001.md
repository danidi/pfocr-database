caption: mTOR signaling pathway and its functions. After receiving the extracellular
  signals, including glucose, amino acids, or growth factor, a series of signaling
  molecules (Ras, PI3K, AKT, and so on) upstream of mTORC1 are activated; then, mTORC1
  and its downstream molecules are activated to regulate a series of cellular processes,
  such as protein synthesis, autophagy, and mitochondrial metabolism. How mTORC2 is
  regulated has not been thoroughly studied. mTORC2 mainly regulates cell survival,
  metabolism, and cytoskeleton organization through SGK1 and PKCα, respectively. mTORC1
  and mTORC2 are linked through AKT.
