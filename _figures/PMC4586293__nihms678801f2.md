caption: 'SIRT1 functions as an intracellular energy sensor to detect the concentration
  of NAD+, and regulates metabolic status by deacetylating various target proteins
  including transcription factors such as FOXO3 and PGC-1α. Dietary energy restriction,
  exercise and phytochemicals such as resveratrol, EGCG, icariin, butein and fisetin
  activate SIRT1. AMPK serves as a detector of cellular energy states that is activated
  when the cellular AMP+ADP)/ATP ratio rises. AMPK and SIRT1 influence each other;
  activation of AMPK increases the intracellular level of NAD+, thus stimulating the
  activity of SIRT1 and, in turn, SIRT1 deacetylates liver kinase B1 (LKB1) which
  can increase the activity of AMPK. AMPK also directly regulates FOXO3. Energy restriction,
  exercise and resveratrol activate the AMPK pathway, leading to increased NAD+ and
  SIRT1 activity. The mTOR pathway up-regulates protein synthesis while inhibiting
  autophagy. IGF-1 activates receptors coupled to the PI3K – Akt pathway which can
  activate mTOR. SIRT1 negatively regulates mTOR signaling pathway. AMPK inhibits
  the activity of mTOR complex either by phosphorylating the regulatory-associated
  protein of mTORC1 or phosphorylation of TSC1/TSC2. Resveratrol, EGCG and curcumin
  decrease both the expression and phosphorylation of Akt/PKB and PI3K. Likewise,
  curcumin, resveratrol and gartanin inhibit phosphorylation of mTOR, S6K1 and 4E-BP1.
  Abbreviations: ADP, adenosine diphosphate; AMP, adenosine monophosphate; ATP, adenosine
  triphosphate; Akt/PKB, protein kinase B; AMPK, adenosine monophosphate activated
  protein kinase; EGCG, (−)-Epigallocatechin-3-gallate; FOXO3, forkhead box protein
  3; IGF-1, insulin-like growth factor 1; LKB1, liver kinase B1; mTOR, mammalian target
  of rapamycin complex 1; NAD+, nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide; PGC-1α, peroxisome
  proliferator-activated receptor gamma coactivator 1-alpha; PI3K, phosphatidylinositol
  3-kinase; Rheb, Ras homolog enriched in brain; SIRT1, sirtuin 1; S6K1, S6 kinase
  1; TSC1/TSC2, tuberous sclerosis protein 1 or 2; 4E-BP1, eukaryotic translation
  initiation factor 4E (eIF4E)-binding protein 1.'
