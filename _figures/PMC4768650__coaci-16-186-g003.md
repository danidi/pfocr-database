caption: Eosinophils contain and/or release a wide array of preformed and de novo
  synthesized mediators important for their effector functions. Specific granules
  contain several cationic proteins, including MBP, ECP, EDN, and EPX. Eosinophils
  can degranulate by exocytosis or by piecemeal degranulation whereby individual granule
  contents are differentially secreted by activated eosinophils without disruption
  of the cell membrane. Sombrero vesicles are morphologically distinct vesicles that
  carry granules to the plasma membrane. Lipid bodies are structurally distinct sites
  within eosinophils that are responsible for synthesis of eicosanoid mediators of
  inflammation []. Eosinophils produce numerous chemokines, cytokines, growth and
  angiogenic factors that mediate allergic inflammation, fibrosis, and thrombosis.
  Eosinophils generate extracellular DNA traps [] and secrete exosomes []. A nonexhaustive
  list of these products is shown in boxes. ECP, eosinophil cationic protein; EDN,
  eosinophil-derived neurotoxin; EPX, eosinophil peroxidase; MBP, major basic protein;
  PAF, platelet activating factor; PDGF, platelet-derived growth factor; SCF, stem
  cell factor; TF, tissue factor; TGF, transforming growth factor; VEGF, vascular
  endothelial growth factor.
