caption: Reversible shifting of hepatocytic Smad3-dependent signaling between tumor-suppression
  and carcinogenesis. During the carcinogenic process in human chronic liver diseases,
  chronic inflammation drives carcinogenesis via a shift from the pSmad3C to the pSmad3L
  pathway. Linker phosphorylation of Smad3 indirectly prevents Smad3C phosphorylation,
  pSmad3C-mediated transcription, and the cytostatic effect of TGF-β/activin upon
  hepatocytes. Inhibition of JNK activity abolishes pSmad3L-mediated oncogenic signaling,
  while restoring the lost tumor-suppressive pSmad3C signaling observed in normal
  hepatocytes
