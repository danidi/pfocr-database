caption: EA ameliorates osteoclastogenesis by inhibiting NF-κB. EA treatment stimulates
  adenosine release to the extracellular environment, resulting in activation of A2AR.
  Activated A2AR inhibits TNF-α resulting in decreased binding of RANKL to RANK and
  finally inhibits osteoclast formation by reducing NF-κB activation. Activated A2AR
  also affects both the PKA and ERK1/2 signaling pathway and thereby inhibits osteoclast
  formation by reducing expression of NF-κB.
