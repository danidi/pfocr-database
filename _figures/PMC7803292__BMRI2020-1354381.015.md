  pathways were majorly involved in the progression of breast cancer. These two pathways
  were regulated by TPD52, miR-124, KLF3, PKCε, and several other downstream effectors.
  The deregulated expression of TPD52, KLF3, miR-124, and PKCε led to the overexpression
  of these two oncogenic pathways which in turn promoted cell survival and proliferation.
  Moreover, the partner genes of KLF3, TPD52, miR-124, and PKCε were extracted through
  STRING software [].
