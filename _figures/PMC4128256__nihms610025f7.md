caption: Models of Ras signaling in NF1-deficient GBM cell lines. A, NF1-deficient,
  MEK inhibitor–sensitive GBM signal through the RAF/MEK/ERK pathway. MEK/ERK inhibition
  by PD0325901 alone decreases cyclin D1 levels and cell growth. B, NF1-deficient,
  MEK inhibitor– resistant GBM signal through both RAF/MEK/ERK and PI3K/mTOR pathways.
  Both PD0325901 and PI-103 are required to decrease cyclin D1 levels. C, NF1-deficient,
  MEK inhibitor–resistant GBM signal through both the RAF/MEK/ERK and PI3K/mTOR pathways
  but ERK cross-talks with the mTOR pathway. PD0325901 and PI-103 may be required
  to decrease mTOR-regulated translation of cyclin D1.
