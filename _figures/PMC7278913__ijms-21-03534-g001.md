caption: TGFβ signaling is activated through BMP or TGFβ ligands. Ligands bind the
  TGFβ receptor II (TβR-II) which recruits and phosphorylates TGFβ receptor I (TβR-I).
  In the canonical TGFβ pathway, TβR-I phosphorylates regulated SMAD (R-SMAD), SMAD2
  and 3, or SMAD1, 5, and 8. SMAD4 forms a heterotrimeric complex with R-SMAD. Once
  translocated to the nucleus, SMAD proteins interact with transcription factors (TF)
  and a co-activator/co-repressor to regulate gene transcription. Signaling is stopped
  through inhibitory SMADs (I-SMAD), SMAD6 and 7, with the help of SMAD ubiquitination
  regulatory factors (Smurfs) 1 and 2. Phosphatase PP2A dephosphorylates receptors
  to stop the signal. Non-canonical TGFβ signaling regroups MAPKs including Erk, p38,
  JNK, PI3k/Akt, and the small GTPase pathway.
