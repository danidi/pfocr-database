caption: 'The schematic representation for the possible interaction of viral oncogene
  proteins with various levels of Wnt/β-catenin cell signaling cascade. (HBV): ()
  hypermethylation of E-cadherin, () SFRP1 and SFRP5 promoters via HBx protein; ()
  dislocating of β-catenin from the destruction complex by binding to APC; () HBx-mediated
  suppression of GSK3β by src kinases, () overexpression of URG7 and the final inactivation
  of GSK3β. (HCV): () HCV core protein mediated-hypermethylation of E-cadherin promoter
  down-regulates E-cadherin expression and thereby β-catenin accumulation; () silencing
  of SFRP1expression by hypermethylation of its promoter; () upregulation of miR-155
  gene and targeting of APC. (HIV): () Tat protein activates PI3K/AKT signaling pathway
  and inactivates GSK3β; () Nef protein compete for the similar site occupied by LEF/TCF
  on β-catenin. (HTLV-1): () Tax protein interacts with DAPLE (disheveled-associating
  protein) to trigger the canonical Wnt pathway; () Tax raise PI3K/Akt activity, resulting
  in the phosphorylation and inactivation of GSK3β. (EBV): () LMP2A activates PI3K/Akt
  pathway, resulting in the phosphorylation and inactivation of GSK3β. () LMP1 inhibits
  Siah1 (an E3 ubiquitin ligase), which is involved in ubiquitination and proteasomal
  degradation of β-catenin; (HPV): () E6 oncoprotein inhibits Siah1, which is involved
  in ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation of β-catenin; () E6 binds to Dsh and
  disrupts the destruction complex (β-catenin stabilization). (HHV-8): () LANA protein
  promotes PI3K/Akt activity, resulting in the phosphorylation and inactivation of
  GSK3β'
