caption: The MUC1-C cytoplasmic domain contains a SAGNGGSSLS motif that binds directly
  to the β-catenin Armadillo repeats (insert) (; ). GSK3β, an effector of the Wnt
  pathway, binds to the upstream STDRSPYEKV site, phosphorylates Ser-44 and blocks
  GSK3β-mediated degradation of β-catenin (; ). Phosphorylation of (i) Tyr-46 by EGFR
  and SRC, and (ii) Thr-41 by PKCδ increases the interaction between MUC1-C and β-catenin
  (insert) (; ; ). Cytoplasmic MUC1-C homodimers bind to β-catenin and stabilize β-catenin
  by blocking GSK3β-mediated phosphorylation and degradation of β-catenin. In the
  nucleus, MUC1-C forms a complex with β-catenin and TCF7L2 on the cyclin D1 promoter
  and coactivates cyclin D1 expression ().
