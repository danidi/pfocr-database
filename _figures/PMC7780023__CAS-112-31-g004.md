caption: Inflammatory signals drive ten‐eleven translocation 2 (TET2) loss‐driven
  clonal expansion and preleukemic myeloproliferation.Tet2‐null hematopoietic stem/progenitor
  cells (HSPCs) show hyperactivation of interleukin‐6 (IL‐6)/Shp2/Stat3/Morrbid pathway,
  which reduces apoptosis and promotes cell survival, clonal expansion, and preleukemic
  myeloproliferation ofTet2‐deficient cells under basal conditions as well as inflammation.
  In addition, noncanonical nuclear factor‐κB signaling mediated by Toll‐like receptor
  (TLR)‐TRAF6‐A20 axis protectsTet2‐deficient HSPCs from chronic inflammation and
  drives competitive advantage of these cells in an inflammatory milieu. BM, bone
  marrow; LPS, lipopolysaccharide
