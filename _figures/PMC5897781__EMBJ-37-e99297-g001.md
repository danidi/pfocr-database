caption: Upon muscle dissociation or injury, skeletal muscle stem cells (MuSCs) exit
  quiescence, become activated, then proliferate and will eventually differentiate
  into fused myofibres. PI3K activation promotes quiescence exit through an AKT‐mTORC1‐Jun
  pathway. Constitutive PI3K signalling leads to activation and differentiation without
  injury while MuSCs fail to exit quiescence in a MuSC‐specific PI3K mutant. FoxO3
  is interacting with PI3K signalling to control quiescence exit. Altogether Wang
  et al () identify a PI3K‐mTORC1‐Jun/FoxO3a signalling axis that is both required
  and sufficient to promote MuSC quiescence exit and early activation.
