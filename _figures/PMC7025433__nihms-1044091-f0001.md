caption: The methionine metabolic pathway. The pathway begins with methionine and
  includes remethylation of homocysteine to methionine and transsulfuration of homocysteine
  to cysteine. Enzymes are shown in red with green arrows indicating their location
  of activity. Cysteine can be incorporated to cysteine sulfinate (via CDO) through
  to taurine or to hydrogen sulfide via CBS. The contribution of cysteine from glutathione
  degradation pathways (GGT) as well as the use of cysteine in glutathione biosynthesis
  is shown. MAT - methionine adenosyltransferase; SAM – S-adenosylmethionine; GNMT
  – glycine N-methyltransferase; SAH – S-adenosylhomocysteine; AHCY – adenosylhomocysteine
  hydrolase; CBS – cystathionine beta-synthase; CTH – cystathionase (aka cystathionine
  gamma-lyase); CDO – cysteine dioxygenase; GS – glutathione synthase; GCL – glutamylcysteine
  ligase; GGT – gamma-glutamyl transpeptidase; GPX – glutathione peroxidase; GR –
  glutathioine reductase; GSH – glutathione; GSSG – glutathione disulfide; H2S – hydrogen
  sulfide; MTR – methionine synthase; BHMT – betaine homocysteine S-methyltransferase;
  THF - tetrahydrofolate.
