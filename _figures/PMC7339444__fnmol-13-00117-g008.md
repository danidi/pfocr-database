caption: The role of Pcdh8 in Wnt/PCP signaling in Xenopus. The extracellular domain
  of Pcdh8 can bind Frizzled, and thus initiate the Wnt/PCP pathway. Pcdh8 sequesters
  Sprouty (Spr), thereby inhibiting its antagonizing effect on the Wnt/PCP pathway.
  In addition, Pcdh8-ANR5 interaction directly activates the effector molecules RhoA
  and JNK. The interaction with Nemo-like kinase 1 (NLK1) stabilizes Pcdh8, thereby
  ensuring continued Wnt/PCP pathway activation. Finally, the interaction with CK2β
  blocks a stabilizing phosphorylation of β-catenin, and ultimately results in decreased
  canonical Wnt signaling.
