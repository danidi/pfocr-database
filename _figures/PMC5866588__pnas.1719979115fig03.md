caption: 'The formation of catharanthine and tabersonine from 4,21 dehydrogeissoshizine
  involves GS, GO, Redox1, Redox2, SAT, an NADPH requiring reaction, HL1, and HL2.
  Pathway intermediates are involved in the elaboration of four major MIA skeletons:
  (i) corynanthean (geissoschizine and isositsirikine), (ii) strychnos (akuammicine,
  stemmadenine, OAS, and DHS), (iii) iboga (catharanthine), and (iv) aspidosperma
  (tabersonine). Pink arrows indicate the major biosynthetic route in C. roseus, and
  dashed arrows indicate hypothetical steps.'
