caption: Schematic of the regulatory mechanism of miRNAs in TLR signaling. Cell surface
  and cytoplasmic TLRs can be regulated by several miRNAs, including let-7 family
  members, miR-21, miR-146, and miR-155. First, miRNAs can bind directly to 3′untranslated
  region of TLRs or TLR-related genes, leading to modulated expression of TLRs through
  posttranscriptional regulation of TLR signaling. Second, miRNAs serve as physiological
  ligands of TLRs, such as miR-21, let-7 family members, and miR-29a, which can activate
  TLR signaling and stimulate the release of inflammatory cytokines and IFN genes
  in some cell types. Functional studies have demonstrated that these miRNAs may participate
  in activation of TLR signaling through regulating the NF-κB pathway and the production
  of inflammatory cytokines, which are shown here.
