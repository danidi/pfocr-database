caption: In metabolic syndrome, hyperglycemia, and hyperlipidemia cause nutrient overinflux
  to kidney cells leading to increased celllular metabolism, which elevates intracelluar
  levels of citrate and ATP. ATP (through the cAMP-PKA pathway) and inflammation (TNF-α)
  synergistically stimulate ACL expression. ACL converts the excess citrate to acetyl-CoA
  to increase nucleocytosolic acetyl-CoA concentration. Acetyl-CoA also provides the
  substrate for histone hyperacetylation, whereby up-regulating key lipogenic and
  fibrogenic genes. The gene products ACC, FAS, and HMGCR promote de novo lipogenesis
  from the excess acetyl-CoA substrate accumulatred in the cytosol leading to ELA
  in the kidney cells, whereas the gene products TGF-β1 and FN stimulate fibrogenesis
  leading to renal fibrosis. FA, fatty acid; TCA, tricarboxylic acid.
