caption: Multiple-injections of lipopolysaccharide (LPS) is effective in preventing
  type 1 diabetes mellitus (type 1 DM). LPS administration suppresses spleen T lymphocyte
  proliferation, increases the generation of T regulatory, reduces the synthesis of
  T-helper 1 pro-inflammatory cytokines [interleukin-2 (IL-2), interleukin-1 (IL-1),
  interferon-γ (IFN-γ), and tumor necrosis factor-α (TNF-α)], and downregulates toll-like
  receptor-4 (TLR-4) and its downstream MyD88-dependent signaling pathway and enhances
  IL-4 and IL-10 and antioxidant defenses. Multiple injections of LPS induce tolerogenic
  dendritic cell (DC) subset with low TLR-4 expression and, thus, prevent development
  of type 1 DM in non-obese diabetic mice [see text and Wang et al. ()].
