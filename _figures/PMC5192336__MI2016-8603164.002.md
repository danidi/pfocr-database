caption: 'MUC induces inflammasome activation. MUC activates the NF-κB pathway through
  TLR2/4, thereby increasing the expressions of pro-IL-1β or pro-IL-18. At the same
  time, MUC induces ROS release from mitochondria. The generated ROS detaches TXNIP
  from thioredoxin and enables TXNIP to interact with the NLRP3 complex. The binding
  of TXNIP to NLRP3 activates inflammasomes, leading to the production of mature IL-1β
  or IL-18. MUC: monosodium urate crystals, TLR: Toll-like receptor, TXNIP: thioredoxin-interacting
  protein, TXR: thioredoxin, and ROS: reactive oxygen species.'
