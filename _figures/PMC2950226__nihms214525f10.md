caption: Proposed model of Dkk3 and Krm in regulating Wnt signaling. (A) Expression
  of Krm has been reported to inhibit Wnt signaling, presumably through endocytosis
  of Dkk1, Krm, and LRP receptors (). (B) Our data suggest that Dkk3 forms complexes
  with Krm proteins in the perinuclear membrane portion of the cell, possibly preventing
  their surface expression. The reduced surface expression of Krm provides a favorable
  environment for Wnt ligands to activate Wnt signaling. The ability of Dkk3 to positively
  modulate the Wnt pathway () does not seem to involve direct interactions or regulation
  of LRP6 receptors. (C) Unlike Dkk3, Dkk1 binds to the LRP6 receptor and induces
  endocytosis (). The reduced surface expression of LRP results in inhibition of Wnt.
  Krm and Dkk1 are Wnt inhibitors (labeled in red). LRP, Fzd, and Dkk3 are Wnt activators
  (labeled in green).
