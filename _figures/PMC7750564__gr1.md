caption: NF-κB signaling pathway is involved in the transcription of HIV which can
  be exploited for HIV cure studies. A. Canonical and noncanonical NF-κB subpathways
  at their resting states. B. Canonical NF-κB activation after PEBP1 knockdown (KO)
  leads to phosphorylation of Raf1 or PKC agonist acts on IKKγ to enable IκBα/ε degradation,
  leading to the activation of HIV transcription or latency reversal. C. Noncanonical
  NF-κB signaling is activated by IAPi/SMACm such as birinapant or AZD5582 via p100
  cleavage into p52 for subsequent HIV transcription or latency reversal.
