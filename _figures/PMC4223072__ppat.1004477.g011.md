caption: The virion envelope gH bind simultaneously αvβ3-integrin and TLR2, and thus
  cross-links them. Signaling by the C-tail of the β3 subunit (green curved arrow)
  results in boosting of the TLR2-dependent pathway – MAL, MYD88 recruitment, IRAK1
  and 4 phosphorylation, and NK-κB activation. Akt serves as a hub, downstream of
  αvβ3-integrin and TLR2, possibly through the prior activation of FAK and PI3K. Src,
  and, downstream, syk, card9, IRF3 are activated by αvβ3-integrin, independently
  of TLR2. The major cellular targets of this pathway are IFN-α, IFN-β, and a specific
  set of cytokines, including IL2 and IL10.
