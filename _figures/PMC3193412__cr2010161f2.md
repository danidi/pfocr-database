caption: The NFκB signaling module consisting of canonical and non-canonical pathways.
  The non-canonical pathway is activated through developmental signals activating
  NIK/IKK1. This activation results in the degradation of IκBδ and processing of p100
  and allows the nuclear translocation of RelA:p50, RelB:p50 and RelB:p52 dimers,
  which activate genes responsible for organ development. The canonical pathway is
  activated through pathogen and inflammatory signals activating NEMO/IKK. This activation
  results in the degradation of IκBα/IκBβ/IκBɛ, allowing for the nuclear translocation
  of RelA:p50, RelA:RelA and cRel:p50 dimers, which then activate genes responsible
  for inflammation and survival.
