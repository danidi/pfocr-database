caption: Possible mechanism of SARS-CoV-2 mediated innate immune response at the lung
  alveolar cell. The SARS-CoV -2 infects alveolar epithelial cells (mainly Alveolar
  epithelial type 1) through ACE2 receptor. Recognition of ssRNA by TLR-7 in the endosomal
  membrane recruits MyD88 to the receptor, which induces proinflammatory cytokines
  and type I IFNs through MyD88-TRAF6-NIK-IKK-NF-Kb pathway and MyD88-TRAF-6-IRF-7
  respectively. The destruction of epithelial cells and the increase of cell permeability
  lead to the release of virus. Phosphorylated IRF-7 and translocate to the nucleus
  results expression of type I IFN genes and release IFNα/β. IkappaB kinase (IKKs)
  directly phosphorylate the inhibitory IκB family members, which normally sequester
  NF-κB as inactive form (NF-κB/ IκB) in the cytosol. However, phosphorylation of
  IκBs leads to their polyubiquitination and degradation via ubiquitin proteasome
  proteolytic mechanism. The activation of the NF-κB pathway results in the induction
  of aberrant inflammatory cytokines and chemokine’s secretion by alveolar cells including
  IL-6, IL-1β, IL-8, CCL2, CCL8 and CXCL9.
