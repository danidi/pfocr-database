caption: The hinge-latch model of Nrf2. Nrf2 is negatively regulated by Keap1 under
  unstressed conditions. Two Keap1 molecules interact with one Nrf2 molecule at two
  binding sites, the ETGE and DLG motifs. Keap1 functions as a substrate adaptor protein
  for the Cullin3 (Cul3)-containing E3-ligase complex and targets Nrf2 for ubiquitination
  and degradation. Under stress conditions, the inhibition of Nrf2 is undermined.
  The de novo synthesized Nrf2 accumulates in the cytoplasm and translocates into
  the nucleus, functioning as a transcriptional factor.
