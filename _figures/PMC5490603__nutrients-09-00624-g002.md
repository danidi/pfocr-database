caption: Pro-inflammatory signaling pathway influences by zinc. Similar to TLR signaling,
  IL-1, and TNF-R signaling pathways converge on a common IκB kinase complex that
  phosphorylates the NF-κB inhibitory protein, resulting in the release of NF-κB and
  its translocation to the nucleus. Zinc prevents the dissociation of NF-κB from its
  corresponding inhibitory protein, thus preventing the nuclear translocation of NF-κB
  and inhibiting subsequent inflammation. Zinc also inhibits IL-6-mediated activation
  of STAT3. Zinc acts as anti-inflammatory element influencing major pro-inflammatory
  signaling pathways.
