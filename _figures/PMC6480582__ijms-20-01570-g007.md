caption: Graphic summary for nifedipine-induced lipogenesis on renal tubular cell.
  In this study, nifedipine upregulated CD36 membrane protein and ROS, but decreased
  the active form of p-AMPK. Further, de novo cholesterol biosynthesis by activating
  nuclear transcriptional factor SREBP-1/2, PPAR-α, and lipin-1 [] were expressed.
  Specifically and selectively, SREBP-1a activates fatty acid and cholesterol synthesis;
  SREBP-1c, fatty acid synthesis; and SREBP-2, cholesterol synthesis and uptake. Then,
  lipogenesis related enzymes ACS, ACC, ACSL1, ACL, and HMGCR were expressed. NDP,
  non-dihydropyridine; HMGCR, HMG CoA reductase; ACS, acetyl CoA synthetase; ACC,
  acetyl CoA carboxylase; ACSL1, long chain fatty acyl elongase; FAS, fatty acid synthase;
  ACL, ATP citrate lyase; PPAR-α, peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-α; SREBP1/2,
  Sterol regulatory element-binding proteins 1/2.
