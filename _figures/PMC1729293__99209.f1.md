caption: Pathways for the metabolism of homocysteine. Normal transsulfuration requires
  cystathionine β synthase with vitamin B6 as cofactor. Remethylation requires 5, 10-methylenetetrahydrofolate
  reductase and methionine synthase. The latter requires folate as cosubstrate and
  vitamin B12 (cobalamin) as cofactor. An alternative remethylation pathway also exists
  using the cobalamin independent betaine-homocysteine methyltransferase.
