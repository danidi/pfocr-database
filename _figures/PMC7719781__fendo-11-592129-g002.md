caption: The mitochondria-associated endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane (MAM) regulates
  cellular calcium homeostasis and insulin signaling. The complex comprising PI3R,
  GRP75, and VDAC is the key mediator of Ca2+ transport from the ER to mitochondria.
  Ca2+ is an important second messenger that regulates various cellular processes.
  First, Ca2+ is required for mitochondrial ATP production. Second, during fasting,
  elevated Ca2+ release from the ER through IP3R and RyR can induce gluconeogenic
  gene expression via the CRCT2/PGC1α pathway. Third, the G protein/IP3/IP3R pathway
  can regulate GLUT4–plasma membrane fusion by modulating Ca2+ levels. Conversely,
  insulin signaling can also promote cellular Ca2+ influx by activating transient
  receptor potential channels (TRPCs).
