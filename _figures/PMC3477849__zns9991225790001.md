caption: Neurofibromin signaling pathway regulation. The NF1 gene product, neurofibromin,
  functions as both a negative regulator of RAS activity (RAS GAP) and as a positive
  regulator of AC activity. Impaired neurofibromin function leads to increased RAS
  activation, resulting in high levels of RAF/MEK and AKT/mTOR signaling. Similarly,
  reduced neurofibromin function is associated with decreased cAMP levels and reduced
  protein kinase A (PKA) activity. GRD, GAP-related domain.
