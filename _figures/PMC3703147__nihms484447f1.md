caption: Upon engaging a cognate receptor complex (IFNAR1-IFNAR2 for Type 1 IFN, IFNGR1-IFNGR2
  for Type 2 IFN or IL28RA-IL10R2 for Type 3 IFN) IFN activate receptor-associated
  JAKs. A resulting tyrosine phosphorylation of STAT proteins leads to the formation
  of transcriptionally active complexes (STAT1 homodimers for all types and additional
  STAT1-STAT2-IRF9 complexes for Types 1/3 IFN) and ensuing induction of ISGs. Cellular
  (β-Trcp, SOCS, SLIM) and diverse viral E3 ubiquitin ligases facilitate the ubiquitination
  of various mediators of IFN signaling pathway to limit the extent and duration of
  this signaling.
