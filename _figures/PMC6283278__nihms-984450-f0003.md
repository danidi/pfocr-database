caption: Cardiomyocyte energy status regulates autophagy via metabolic signalling.
  Under substrate deficit conditions or oxidative stress, decreased ATP levels stimulate
  5′-AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) activity and, therefore, autophagy via activation
  of serine/threonine-protein kinase ULK1 and downstream apoptosis regulator BCL-2
  and beclin 1 through inhibition of autophagy suppressors (such as mechanistic target
  of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1)). At the same time, growth or cell survival stimuli
  activate insulin or insulin-like growth factor I (IGF1) signalling in cardiomyocytes,
  leading to the induction of the insulin–RACα serine/threonine-protein kinase (AKT1)
  pathway. Activation of GTP-binding protein RHEB results in inhibition of autophagy
  by autophagy suppressors (primarily mTORC1) and transcription factors related to
  lysosomal biogenesis (such as transcription factor EB (TFEB)). ROS, reactive oxygen
  species.
