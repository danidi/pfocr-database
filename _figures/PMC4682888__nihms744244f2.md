caption: Integration of Akt and mTORC1 signaling into the IL-4R and TLR4 pathways.
  A) In parallel to the canonical Jak-Stat pathway, the IL-4R activates the PI3K-Akt-TSC-mTORC1
  signaling pathway. B) TLR4 engages the adaptor protein BCAP to activate the PI3K-Akt-TSC-mTORC1
  signaling pathway in parallel to the canonical pathway that culminates in activation
  of NF-kB and IRFs. Independent of its role in the PI3K-Akt-TORC1 pathway, Akt can
  be activated by canonical TLR4 signaling via TBK/IKKε. Additionally, other kinases
  activated by TLR4 signaling can regulate mTORC1 activity via phosphorylation of
  the TSC complex (e.g. Erk and IKKβ). Such integration of Akt and mTORC1 signaling
  into canonical signaling allows for coordinate control of cellular metabolism and
  function during M1 and M2 activation.
