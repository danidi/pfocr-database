caption: Nutrient, growth factor, and energy sensing impinge on the mTOR pathway.
  Both TSC1 and TSC2 are major pathway components in the mTOR signaling cascade. TSC
  is caused by mutations in either the TSC1 or TSC2 genes that interact in a protein-protein
  signaling complex to negatively regulate cell growth. Nutrients, growth factor,
  and energy status signal to the rapamycin-sensitive TORC1, while TORC2 is known
  to fully activate Akt.
