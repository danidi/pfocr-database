caption: Displayed is the signaling pathway associated with insulin-mediated activation
  of endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS). Binding of insulin to the insulin receptor
  leads to the phosphorylation of the insulin receptor substrate (IRS) and activation
  of phosphoinositide-3 kinase (PI3K) and Akt. Akt, in turn, phosphorylates eNOS.
  In the setting of diabetes mellitus activation of PKCβ leads to the activation of
  NFκB, blocks insulin signaling and reduces synthesis of nitric oxide (NO)
