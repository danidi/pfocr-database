caption: The proposed mechanisms for the reciprocal repression between calcineurin
  and miR-133 in hypertrophic heart. (A) Either exogenous miR-133 supplement or endogenous
  miR-133 increase gives rise to the transcriptional repression of calcineurin and
  suppression of NFAT, causing the increased miR-133 level. Eventually, the increased
  miR-133 further enhanced miR-133 expression. (B) The calcineurin/ NFAT signaling
  is activated by pathological stimuli, giving rise to the downregulation of miR-133
  expression. The decreased miR-133 induces the enhancement of calcineurin transcription.
  Consequently, the activated calcineurin triggers the further increase of calcineurin/
  NFAT pathway. ↑, increase; ↓, decrease.
