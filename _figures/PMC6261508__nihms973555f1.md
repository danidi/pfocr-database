caption: Downstream effects of mTORC1, which include modulation of cell growth, proliferation,
  metabolism and ion-channel expression, are primarily mediated by ribosomal biogenesis
  (proteins S6K and S6) and protein translation (4E-BP1 and eIF4E). mTORC2 also has
  effects on cell growth and function, but unlike mTORC1, is not inhibited by the
  drug rapamycin, at least with short-term use. Regulation of cell growth and maintenance
  in different energy states is mediated by upstream regulators of mTORC1, including
  the hamartin/tuberin complex encoded by TSC1 and TSC2 genes, the PI3K/AKT pathway,
  the GATOR1 complex pathway and the STRADα/AMPK pathway. Genetic mutations affecting
  function of these regulatory pathways have been shown to cause mTOR hyperactivation
  in animal models and human tissues. Dysregulation of mTOR cell signaling is, therefore,
  thought to be a common mechanism for cortical developmental malformations and symptomatic
  epilepsy seen in a diverse group of neuroclinical syndromes.
