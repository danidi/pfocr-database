caption: In the presence of amino acids, growth factors and energy, the mTOR complex
  represses autophagy by inhibiting the kinase activity of ULK1. The PI3K/Akt pathway
  inhibits autophagy, while AMPK activates autophagy by controlling mTOR activation
  under nutrient-limiting conditions. Upon autophagy induction (1), the ULK1 complex
  activates the BECN1/VPS34 complex to initiate (2) phagophore formation and nucleation.
  BECN1 can be activated directly by the IRE1α/JNK pathway or inhibited by the pharmacological
  drug 3-methyladenine (3-MA). Phagophore elongation (3) proceeds to engulf and sequester
  autophagic cargo and the phagophore membrane acquires LC3. Ubiquitin-like conjugation
  systems mediate the closure of the autophagosome (4). Maturation of the autophagosome
  (5) occurs via fusion with late endocytic/lysosomal compartments, forming the autolysosome
  where material is degraded. Autolysosomes are then recycled in a process that allows
  for lysosome reformation (6).
