caption: M1 macrophages appear to promote carcinogenesis in the bladder. This occurs
  through activation of the Stat3 pathway as well as MMP-9 expression, resulting in
  EMT and basement membrane disruption respectively. M2 macrophages have varying effects
  on bladder tumors depending on their microenvironment. In hypoxic conditions M2
  cells promote the increased production of VEGF, TNF-α, COX-2, IL-1β and MIF, which
  improve BCG outcomes. Alternatively, under normoxic conditions M2 cells inhibit
  IL-12, IL-18, IL-1β and TNF-α secretion, resulting in immunosuppression.
