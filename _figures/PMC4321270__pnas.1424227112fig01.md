caption: Role of parasite Pfs47 in evasion from mosquito JNK-mediated immune responses.
  Ookinete invasion by NF54 (WT) P. falciparum parasites results in the suppression
  of JNK activation, ultimately leading to increased parasite survival. In the absence
  of Pfs47 (Pfs47 KO), ookinete invasion activates the JNK pathway. Downstream events
  including caspase activation and epithelial nitration effectively “mark” invading
  parasites for recognition and lysis by the mosquito complement-like system.
