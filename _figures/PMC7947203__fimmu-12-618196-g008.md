caption: Schematic illustration of how HBsAg interferes with the nuclear factor kappa
  B (NF-κB) signaling pathway. HBsAg specifically binds to TAK1 and TAB2, suppresses
  the polyubiquitination of TAK1-TAB2 complex, blocks TAK1 and IKBα phosphorylation,
  inhibits the NF- κB subunits p50 and p65 translocation from cytoplasm to nucleus,
  and downregulates the expression of IFNs and inflammatory cytokines. On the other
  hand, HBsAg interferes the molecular interaction between TAK1 and TAB2 with promoting
  autophagic degradation, further inhibiting the activation of NF-κB signaling pathways.
