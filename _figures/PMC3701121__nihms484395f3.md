caption: 'Angiogenesis signaling pathways in epilepsy. Proposed vascular endothelial
  growth factor (VEGF) pro-angiogenic pathways: (i) Inositol trisphosphate (IP3),
  endothelial nitric oxide synthase (eNOS), and phospholipase C (PLC) induce the proteolysis
  of the basement membrane by MMP-2 and MMP-9. (ii) PLC and P42/44-MAP kinase promote
  endothelial cells (EC) proliferation and migration. (iii) Src, PI3K, and p38MAP
  kinase degrade the tight junctions. VEGF neuroprotective pathways: (i): Local perfusion
  is improved by PKC-mediated ECs proliferation, whereas eNOS affords a rapid adaptation
  of local blood flow to neuron energetic demand. (ii) VEGFR-2 expressed by neurons
  provides an autocrine protection via the anti-apoptotic PI3K pathway. Angiopoietins
  effects: Ang1, released by pericytes is a constitutive ligand of Tie2, enabling
  cell maturation and protections via PI3-K/Akt. On the contrary, the inducible Ang2,
  released by endothelial Weibel–Palade bodies, acts as a Tie2 antagonist worsening
  VEGF effects on the vasculature. The ratio Ang1/Ang2 has an impact on vascular permeability.'
