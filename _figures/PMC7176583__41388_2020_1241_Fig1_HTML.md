caption: The MEK–ERK and FAK signaling pathways are activated by phosphorylated CagA
  to mediate hummingbird phenotype of the epithelial cells and promote cell migration.
  The β-catenin is activated by nonphosphorylated intracellular CagA by disruption
  of the E-cadherin–β-catenin complexes or PI3K–AKT signaling. CagA activates JAK-STAT3
  pathway by releasing IL-6/IL11 and activating gp130. Nuclear translocation of STAT3
  initiates gene expression for cell proliferation. H. pylori peptidoglycan and VacA
  potentiate PI3K–AKT signaling to promote epithelial cell migration, increase proliferation,
  and reduce apoptosis. CagA, VacA, and peptidoglycan coordinate to activate NF-κB
  signaling cascade thus to transcriptionally upregulate proinflammatory cytokines
  such as IL-1 and IL-8, and promote inflammation.
