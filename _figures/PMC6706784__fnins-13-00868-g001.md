caption: Insulin signaling regulates neuronal function. Insulin binding to its receptor,
  through the intracellular substrates IRSs, leads to activation of PI3-K pathway
  which, in turn, inhibits GSK3, mTORC1 and IDE, reducing alpha-synuclein aggregation,
  and enhances cell survival. In addition, insulin-induced PI3K activation stimulates
  dopamine synthesis and turnover and mitochondrial biogenesis. This figure includes
  experimental results obtained in cell cultures and partially confirmed in rodent
  and human brain.
