  type-2 receptors (AT2Rs) and dopamine -1 receptors (D1Rs) interact in the control
  of Na+ transport. D1Rs activate adenylyl cyclase (AC) generating intracellular cyclic
  AMP (cAMP), which acts via protein kinase A (PKA) to induce translocation of intracellular
  AT2Rs across a microtubule network to the plasma membrane. AT2R translocation is
  required for D1R- induced natriuresis.
