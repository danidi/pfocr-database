caption: TSC2 mediated crosstalk between the Akt/mTOR pathway and the cyclin D1-CDK4/6
  axis.The cyclinD1/CDK4/6 axis is responsible for increased phosphorylation TSC2
  and TSC1, and promotes the phosphorylation of the mTOR substrates, 4E-BP1 and S6K1.
  Additionally, EGFR/HER2 (via Akt) triggers TSC2 phosphorylation, and the combination
  of CDK4/6is and HER2 inhibitors may suppress mTORC1 activity more than either drug
  alone; Additionally, greater suppression of mTORC1 activity may relive feedback
  inhibition of EGFR family kinases. This may render HER2+ breast cancer cells more
  sensitive to the effects of EGFR/HER2 inhibitors.Akt, protein-kinase B; CDK4/6,
  cyclin-dependent kinase 4/6; CDK4/6is, CDK4/6 inhibitors; EGFR, epithelial growth-factor
