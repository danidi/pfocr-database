caption: The cellular effects of extracellular ADO in the regulation of lymphocyte
  functions. After A2AR stimulation, regulatory T cells (Tregs) increased proliferation
  and expression of CTLA-4, CD39, and CD73, and inhibited CD8+ T cell proliferation
  []. CD4+ T cells decreased the production of IL-2, TNFα, IL-6, and IFNγ, and enhanced
  the production of IL-10 [,,]. In NK cells, ADO suppresses the production of IL-2,
  TNF-α, IFN-γ, and granulocyte macrophage colony-stimulating factor (GM-CSF) and
  blocks their cytolytic activity []. In CD8+ T cells, ADO suppresses the production
  of IL-2 and IFN-γ and blocks their cytolytic activity [,].
