caption: 'Schematic representation of relevant events involving IRE1α and PERK proteins
  at mitochondria–ER contacts (MERCs). (A) PERK-involving events at MERCs: (i) PERK
  tethers the ER to mitochondria promoting the rapid transfer of reactive oxygen species
  (ROS) signals, likely under the form of lipid hydroperoxides (); (ii) Mitofusin
  2 (Mfn2) lies upstream of PERK and under basal conditions maintains PERK inactive
  (); the PERK/eIF2α/ATF4 signaling pathway upregulates the expression of SERCA1 truncated
  proteins (S1T) (iii) and sigma-1 receptor (σ1R) (iv) at MERCs (; ). (B) Events involving
  IRE1α at MERCs: (i) σ1R-dependent stabilization of IRE1α oligomerization at MERCs
  (); (ii) mitochondrial ubiquitin ligase (MITOL)-dependent ubiquitylation of IRE1α
  at MERCs prevents ER stress induced apoptosis (); (iii) the AKT-mammalian target
  of rapamycin (mTOR) signaling attenuates IRE1 RNase activity by promoting the re-establishment
  of ER-mitochondria contacts (); (iv) IRE1α scaffolds IP3R at MERCs to sustain calcium
  transfer and mitochondrial bioenergetics (). The putative pro-survival or pro-apoptotic
  outputs of the depicted events are reported.'
