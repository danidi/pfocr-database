caption: EGT biosynthetic pathway in M. tuberculosis. A, EGT biosynthesis occurs through
  five enzymatic steps, and the genes encode for a γ-glutamylcysteine synthase (EgtA),
  a formylglycine-generating enzyme-like protein (EgtB), a glutamine amidotransferase
  (EgtC), a methyltransferase (EgtD), and a pyridoxal 5-phosphate protein (EgtE).
  The pathway proceeds from l-histidine through the intermediary precursor hercynine,
  hercynyl γ-glutamylcysteine sulfoxide, and hercynylcysteine sulfoxide. EGT acquires
  its sulfur from γ-glutamylcysteine. B, in silico analysis identified the gene cluster
  Rv3700c–Rv3704c to encode for EGT biosynthesis in M. tuberculosis. PLP, pyridoxal
  5-phosphate.
