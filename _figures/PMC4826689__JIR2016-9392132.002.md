caption: Noncanonical Wnt signaling pathway (β-catenin-independent Wnt signaling pathway).
  Noncanonical Wnt ligand (such as the Wnt5a, a typical noncanonical Wnt) binds to
  its receptor (Fzd) and coreceptor (Ror1/2) and triggers the noncanonical signaling
  cascades, which includes the Wnt/Calcium (Ca2+) and Wnt/planar cell polarity (PCP)
  pathways. In the Wnt/Ca2+pathway (left panel), Wnt protein binds to Fzd and Ror2
  receptor and leads to activate G proteins, resulting in enhancing the intracellular
  calcium levels, or decreases cGMP; the calcium/calmodulin-dependent protein kinase
  II (CaMKII) or protein kinase C (PKC) was then activated. In the Wnt/PCP pathway
  (right panel), Wnt proteins bind to Fzd receptors on the cell surface followed by
  activating Rho/Rac small GTPase and Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK) to assist with cytoskeletal
  organization and gene expression.
