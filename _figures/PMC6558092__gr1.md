caption: PD-1 signaling pathway in T cells.When engaged with a ligand, PD-1 becomes
  phosphorylated at its cytoplasmic tyrosine residue, leading to binding of protein
  tyrosine phosphatases (PTPs), such as SHP2. SHP2 can dephosphorylate kinases and
  antagonize the positive signals that occur through TCR and CD28 receptors, resulting
  in inhibition of effort T cell function and T cell exhaustion. Additionally, PD-1
  can inhibit T cell functions by increasing the expression of transcription factors
  such as BATF, which can further counter the signals downstream of TCR and CD28 co-stimulation.
