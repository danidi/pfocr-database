caption: Role of IRS-1 in insulin and IGF-1 signaling. Upon ligand binding to the
  receptor, IRS-1 is recruited to the receptor and phosphorylated on tyrosine residues,
  which serve as docking sites for adaptor proteins of the PI3K/Akt pathway or Ras-Erk
  MAPK pathway. Akt signaling is restrained by a negative feedback loop via mTOR and
  its effector serine/threonine kinase S6K. Phosphorylation of multiple serine residues
  on IRS-1 by mTOR/S6K may create a phosphodegron required for CUL7 E3 ligase-mediated
  ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation.
