caption: Metabolic regulation in GM-CSF-BMDCs. (A) Short-term (minutes to several
  hours after TLR ligation) metabolic regulation in GM-CSF-BMDCs, which undergo rapid
  metabolic reprogramming controlled by TBK1-IKK    -Akt-HKII signaling. This signaling
  pathway promotes glycolysis and subsequently fuels other metabolic activities, such
  as fatty acid synthesis and lipid production, thereby supporting DC activation.
  (B) Long-term (over 14 h after TLR ligation) metabolic regulation in GM-CSF-BMDCs.
  TLR ligation triggers multiple signaling pathways, including PI3K-Akt, mTORC1, HIF1α,
  AMPK, and iNOS. TLR-triggered PI3K-Akt, mTORC1-HIF1α, and AMPK pathways promote
  glycolysis, which is antagonized by IL-10, whereas iNOS-NO suppresses OXPHOS.
