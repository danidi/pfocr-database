caption: The ZNRF1 signaling pathway mediates oxidative stress–induced neuronal apoptosis
  and axonal degeneration. Oxidative stress induced by treatment with 6OHDA or H2O2
  or generated by NADPH oxidases in axons upon traumatic injury activates EGFR tyrosine
  kinase activity and leads to ZNRF1 phosphorylation at Y103. This stimulates the
  E3 ubiquitin ligase activity of ZNRF1, which ubiquitinates and targets AKT for degradation
  via the UPS. Degradation of AKT relieves the inhibitory phosphorylation of GSK3β,
  which then phosphorylates CRMP2 and subjects it to degradation. In axons, CRMP2
  is required for microtubule stabilization, whose disassembly results in axonal degeneration.
  In soma, oxidative stress activates this same ZNRF1 signaling pathway, which causes
  cleavage and activation of caspase 3, leading to neuronal apoptosis.
