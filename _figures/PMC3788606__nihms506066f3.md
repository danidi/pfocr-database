caption: TGF-β signaling. TGF-β binds and activates its receptor (TGFBR). In the canonical
  pathway, the active complex proceeds to phosphorylate Smad 2 and Smad 3 proteins,
  which recruit Smad 4 to form a Smad2/3-Smad4 complex. This complex translocates
  to the nucleus, activating the transcription of TGF-β target genes that affect cell
  proliferation, cell death, and extracellular matrix (ECM) destruction. TGF-β signaling
  can be antagonized at the receptor level by Smad 7. The proto-oncoproteins Ski and
  SnoN also negatively regulate the TGF-β signaling by directly interacting with the
  Smad2/3-Smad4 complex.
