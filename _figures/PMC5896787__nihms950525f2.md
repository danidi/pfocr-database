caption: Upon TCR and CD28 engagement, CTLA-4 is rapidly up-regulated and binds with
  high affinity to B7 molecules. This leads to the CTLA-4 cytoplasmic tail recruitment
  of tyrosine phosphatases (SHP-1, SHP-2 and PP2a) that switch off TCR/CD28 downstream
  signaling pathways, otherwise promoting cell proliferation, differentiation, motility,
  acquisition of effector functions and glycolysis. On the other hand, PD-1 engagement
  induces LCK to phosphorylate its cytoplasmic tail with the consequent recruitment
  of tyrosine phosphatases (in particular SHP-2), which, besides down-regulating TCR/CD28
  activation, reduces phosphorylation of PD-1 itself. PD-1 downstream signaling pathway
  also leads to Cpt1a up-regulation and promotes fatty acid oxidation (FAO).
