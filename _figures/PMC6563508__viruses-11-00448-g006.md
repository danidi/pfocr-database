caption: Interactions between viruses, RNA interference (RNAi), and the interferon
  pathway in mammals. Virus infection induces the expression of type I interferons,
  leading to the expression of Interferon stimulated genes (ISGs) that collectively
  restrict virus infection. The interferon pathway inhibits RNAi via multiple mechanisms,
  whereas miRNAs inhibit expression of ISGs. Virus infection induces an antiviral
  RNAi response under specific conditions, in stem cells or in absence of viral suppressors
  of RNAi.
