caption: Cholesterol is transported into Leydig cells via lipoprotein (LP)-receptor
  (SCARB1). When LH binds to luteinizing hormone receptor (LHCGR) to induce LHCGR-complex
  interaction leading to cAMP cascade, the latter activates protein kinase A, which
  induces the expression of steroidogenic acute regulatory protein (STAR). STAR is
  the rate-limiting step to transport intracellular cholesterol into mitochondrial
  inner membrane. P450 cholesterol side chain cleavage enzyme (CYP11A1); 3β-hydroxysteroid
  dehydrogenase 1 (HSD3B1); 17α-hydroxylase/C17,20-lyase (CYP17A1); 17β-hydroxysteroid
  dehydrogenase 3 (HSD17B3); 5α-reductase 1 (SRD5A1); 3α-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase
  (HSD3A).
