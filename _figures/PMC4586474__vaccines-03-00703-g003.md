caption: 'The TGF-β-IDO-SHP axis. The TGF-β-IDO-SHP axis activates the non-canonical
  NF-κB pathway []. In splenocytes, SHP-1 inhibits the protein kinase IRAK1 and tips
  the balance of activation of the canonical versus non-canonical NF-κB signaling
  pathway in favor of the latter, resulting in upregulated production of type I interferon
  []. In addition to TGF-β, the aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) has also been shown
  to play a role in IDO production [,]. Interestingly, kynurenines produced from IDO’s
  enzymatic activity can also bind and activate as an endogenous ligand, AhR [,].
  Abbreviations: IFN (α β, γ,): interferon alpha, beta, gamma; TGF-β: transforming
  growth factor-beta; TNF: tumor necrosis factor; TLR4: toll-like receptor 4; TRAF:
  TNF-receptor associated factors; TRIF: TIR-domain-containing adapter-inducing interferon-β;
  SHP: orphan nuclear receptor small heterodimer partner; SMAD: extracellular signal
  transducers from TGF-β ligands to the nucleus; IRF (1, 3): and interferon regulatory
  factor 1,3.'
