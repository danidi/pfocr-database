caption: a Schematic diagrams demonstrate gradual evolution of network topology of
  the Notch-1/β-catenin axis in prosimians, simians, Hominoidae and Homininae. Note
  that sophistication of the topology parallels enhances signalling activity of the
  Wnt/β-catenin in higher primates. b Schematic diagrams (top) demonstrate gradual
  evolution of the network topology by recruitment of additional network motifs, into
  the existing circuitry of Notch-1/β-catenin axis. Evolution of the microRNA radically
  alters the existing genetic network. Evolution of miRNA-4673 in Homininae generates
  a type-4 coherent feed-forward loop (C4-FFL) that amplifies activity of the Wnt/β-catenin
  (bottom). Simultaneously, inclusion of Notch-1 in a type-1 incoherent feed-forward
  loop (I1-FFL) formed by the miRNA reduces the signalling activity of the notch signalling
  pathway (bottom).
