caption: Major pathways of cancer immunotherapy of Ganoderma in immune cells. (A)
  GLPS induces NF-κB activation and p38 mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) phosphorylation
  in DC. GLPS might activate T cells via inositol triphosphate/Ca2+ (IP3/Ca2+) and
  protein kinase C (PKC) pathways. (B) F3 induces the expression of Blimp-1mRNA through
  p38 MAPK pathway and mediates intracellular signal through NF-κB pathway in B cell.
  The water extract of G. lucidum activates NK cells by the mechanism of activating
  NKG2D/NCR receptors and MAPK signaling pathway. (C) The polysaccharide of G. atrum
  induced macrophage activation through MAPK (JNK, ERK1/2) and NF-κB signaling pathways.
