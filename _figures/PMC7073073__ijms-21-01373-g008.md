caption: Representation of ATF6 acting on the TGFβ–Smad pathway for induction of fibrosis
  genes. TGFβ is a ligand for TGFβ receptor II, which upon binding forms a receptor
  complex with TGFβ receptor I, activating their kinase function. The TGFβ receptor
  complex phosphorylates receptor-Smads 2 and 3, which then complex with co-Smad 4
  and move to the nucleus where they activate fibrosis genes (black arrows). These
  data suggest ATF6 activity inhibits the phosphorylation of the receptor-Smads at
  or before the level of the TGFβ chemical inhibitor SB431542 (red “T” arrows).
