caption: Schematic diagram illustrating D1R and NMDAR signaling cascades in heterozygous
  Nf1 mice. In the striatum, dopamine activates D1R leading to cAMP formation and
  PKA activation that in turn leads to ERK1/2 phosphorylation. In addition, stimulation
  of NMDAR promotes Ras activation through Ras-GRF1 which catalyzes the conversion
  of inactive Ras-GDP to active Ras-GTP. This process is counteracted by neurofibromin,
  which diminishes Ras activity. However, in heterozygosis (Nf1+/−) the balance between
  Ras-GTP and Ras-GDP is displaced towards the active form, favoring ERK1/2 phosphorylation
  while cAMP levels are reduced.
