caption: The TLR2/1 signaling cascade and respective regulatory nodes. αSyn binding
  to the TLR2/1 heterodimer leads to a MyD88-dependent response that stimulates the
  kinase activity of the IRAK complex. The IRAK complex in turn activates TRAF6 K63-linked
  auto-ubiquitination, which subsequently leads to the release of the IKKs and activation
  of TAK1. The IKKs will designate IκBα for degradation and TAK1 will stimulate the
  MAPK pathway leading to the NF-κB, JNK, and p38 nuclear translocation to upregulate
  proinflammatory cytokines. There are several potential regulatory nodes and letters
  A-F represent those targets for intervention along the signaling pathway in order
  to impede pathogenesis.
