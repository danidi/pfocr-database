caption: Inhibition of the de novo pyrimidine biosynthesis by blocking the activity
  of DHODH leads to reduced pyrimidine nucleotide availability and thus affects the
  synthesis of pre-rRNA and rRNA. This in turns induces nucleolar stress leading to
  changes in nucleolar morphology, the stabilization of p53, cell cycle arrest and
  cell death. Furthermore, brequinar and TMZ treatment leads to decreased expression
  of the DNA damage repair enzyme MGMT. The decrease in MGMT levels leads to an increase
  in H2AX phosphorylation and thus enhances the sensitivity of glioblastoma cells
  to DNA damage-induced death.
