caption: Cancer–immunity cycle. When tumor-infiltrating APCs recognize tumor antigens,
  they get activated and thus migrate to secondary lymphoid organs. There, the APC
  interacts and promotes the priming and activation of antigen-specific-naïve T cells.
  Then, these active T cells, when infiltrated to the tumors through blood vessels,
  can recognize tumor antigens and thus kill the antigen-expressing cancer cells.
  Cytotoxic T cells kill their targets via either the death receptor pathway or the
  granule exocytosis pathway. ER = endoplasmic reticulum; APC = antigen presenting
  cell; MHC = major histocompatibility complex molecule; TCR = T cell receptor; TAP
  = transporter associated with antigen processing.
