caption: Growth factors binding to their cell surface receptors activate guanine exchange
  factors (GEF) such as SOS (son of sevenless) that are attached by the adaptor protein
  GRB2 (growth-factor-receptor bound protein 2). SOS stimulates the release of bound
  GDP from RAS, and it is exchanged for GTP, leading to the active RAS-GTP conformation.
  The GTPase-activating proteins (GAP) can bind to RAS-GTP and accelerate the conversion
  of RAS-GTP to RAS-GDP (guanosine diphosphate), which terminates signaling. Mutated
  RAS is constitutively active in the RAS-GTP conformation. Activated RAS regulates
  multiple cellular functions through effectors including the Raf-MEK-ERK pathway,
  PI3K, RALGDS, RALGDS-like gene (RLG) and RGL2.
