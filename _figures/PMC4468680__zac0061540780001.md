caption: CpxRA two-component signal transduction system (). In response to membrane
  stress, CpxP dissociates from CpxA. CpxA phosphorylates on a conserved histidine
  residue and donates a phosphate group to a conserved aspartic acid residue on CpxR
  (pathway 1, left). A PcpxP-lacZ transcriptional fusion serves as a reporter for
  CpxR activity. In response to glucose, CpxR can accept phosphoryl groups from acetyl
  phosphate (AcP), and transcription requires the acetylation of RNA polymerase (RNAP)
  and YfiQ (pathway 2, right) (). Compounds that activate CpxRA might target CpxP,
  NlpE, CpxA, CpxR, or YfiQ ().
