caption: The stimulation of cells to increase intracellular Ca2+ leads to activation
  of MLCK and phosphorylation of myosin II RLC. RLC phosphorylation increases actin-associated
  myosin II ATPase activity and provides the motor activity to translocate a TNFR-1-associated
  vesicle from the Golgi to the plasma membrane or within the plasma membrane, where
  TNF binds and receptor trimerization occurs. Activated TNFR-1 recruits TRADD, FADD,
  and pro-caspase-8. Continued TNF stimulation may amplify or prolong increased intracellular
  Ca2+signaling by activating membrane calcium channels.
