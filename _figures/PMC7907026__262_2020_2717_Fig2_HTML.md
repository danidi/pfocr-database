caption: Diverse roles of the immune checkpoint in tumor cells that drive to the stimulation
  or inhibition of metastasis. Inhibitory immune checkpoints including PD-1, B7-H7,
  TIM-3, PI3Kγ, CD73, and CD 47 play monumental roles during metastasis; these immune
  checkpoints employ diverse mechanisms such as IL-8 formation, EMT stimulation, and
  T cell deterioration. B7-H7 and CD73 induce their promoting role through IL-8 formation
  during metastasis and the common mechanism of CD73 and A2AR is PI3K/AKT signaling
  pathway to induce metastasis. The expression of inhibitory immune checkpoints such
  as CTLA-4, BTLA, and A2AR on tumor cells can promote metastasis while, CD40 expression
  which is a costimulatory immune checkpoint cripple metastasis via affecting the
  NK and T cells functions and the synthesis of cytokines
