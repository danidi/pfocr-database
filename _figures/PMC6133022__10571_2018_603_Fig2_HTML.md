caption: Effects of HIV infection on neurons’ α7-nAChRs. 1–4 gp120 released from HIV-infected
  microglia interact with CXCR4 expressed by neurons, activating the Ras/Raf/MEK/Erk/Egr-1
  signaling pathway that eventually promotes α7-nAChR upregulation in neuronal cells
  (Ballester et al. ). Moreover, this response can be recapitulated by adding SDF-1α,
  the endogenous agonist of CXCR4, which also induces α7-nAChR overexpression in neuronal
  cells (Ballester et al. ). These responses are sensitive to CXCR4 blockade with
  AMD3100 and MEK inhibition by U0126 or PD98059. 5 Furthermore, α7-nAChR activation
  with ACh promotes intracellular Ca2+ overload that results in cell death and apoptosis
  (Ballester et al. ; Capó-Vélez et al. ). 6 The gp120-induced α7-nAChR upregulation
  described in points 1–5 involves a decrease in dupα7 expression, its repressive
  partial duplication (Ramos et al. )
