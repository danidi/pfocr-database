caption: Model of cyanide assimilation in plants. CAS converts cyanide released from
  the cyanogen linamarin, to cyanoalanine plus hydrogen sulfide in the presence of
  cysteine. Cyanoalanine is converted by a nitrilase to asparagine, which is then
  converted to aspartate and ammonia by asparaginase. In this way, cyanide nitrogen
  can be incorporated into the free amino acid pool of the plant. An alternative pathway
  (red arrow) can potentially result in cyanide detoxified to thiocyanate by rhodanese.
