caption: Inhibitory nucleotide-binding oligomerization domain (NOD)-like receptors
  put the brake on NF-κB activation via canonical and non-canonical pathways. Both
  NLRX1 and NLRP12 inhibit the activation of NF-κB canonical pathway following toll-like
  receptor (TLR) stimulation. Nlrx1 interacts and inhibits TNF receptor-associated
  factor (TRAF) 6, while NLRP12 inhibits the phosphorylation of IRAK-1. NLRP12 can
  also inhibit non-canonical NF-κB signaling through regulation of TRAF3 and NF-κB
  inducing kinase (NIK).
