caption: Dendritic cells initiate T cell immunity in the lymph nodes. A mature DC
  encounters antigen in the lymph nodes where it processes and cross-presents the
  antigen in the context of peptide/MHC molecules to T cells. A T cell bearing a receptor
  (TCR) of cognate specificity to the presented peptide/MHC is induced by TCR stimulation
  (signal 1) to express CD40L, leading to activation of the DC through CD40 engagement.
  DC activation results in increased costimulation to the T cell through the B7/C28
  pathway (signal 2). TCR stimulation in the presence of CD28 signaling results in
  T cell activation, IL-2 dependent proliferation, and differentiation into an effector
  T cell. The cytokine milieu directs T cell differentiation along one of the helper
  T cell (TH) lineages.
