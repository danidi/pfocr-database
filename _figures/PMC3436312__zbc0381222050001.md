caption: 'Biosynthetic pathway of GPL. A, shown is organization of GPL gene cluster
  in M. smegmatis. B, shown is a schematic representation of the modular organization
  of PKS and non-ribosomal peptide synthetase (NRPS) of GPL cluster of M. smegmatis.
  Bimodular GPL-PKS contains different functional domains: ACP, KS, AT, ketoreductase
  (KR) in module I along with dehydratase (DH) and enoyl reductase (ER) in module
  II. The PKS product from module II is then transferred sequentially onto the non-ribosomal
  peptide synthetase modules (MPS1 and MPS2) of the GPL cluster, leading to the formation
  of peptidolipid backbone that undergoes further modifications to form GPLs.'
