caption: Schematic diagram of partial action pathways of H2S donors and their derivatives
  on TNBC. H2S donors and their derivatives participate in the regulation of multiple
  pathways to induce apoptosis and block the invasion, proliferation and metastasis
  of TNBC cells by triggering the release of H2S in response to specific stimuli.
  They not only promote TNBC cells apoptosis by activating mitochondrial apoptosis
  pathway and inhibiting phosphorylation or expression of related proteins involved
  in NF-κB, PI3K/Akt/mTOR, Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK signaling pathways, but also ameliorate
  aberrant activation of the β-catenin pathway, followed by MMP-2/9 activity inhibition
  and EMT reversal.
