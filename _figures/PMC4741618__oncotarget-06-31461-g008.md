caption: Self-assembling nanoparticles encapsulating zoledronic acid (NZ) inhibit
  the farnesyl pyrophosphate synthase (FPPS) step in the mevalonate (Mev) pathway.
  So doing, NZ reduces the Ras/ERK1/2/HIF-1α-mediated transcription of P-glycoprotein
  (Pgp), the transcription of glycolytic genes, the synthesis of ubiquinone (UQ) and
  the mitochondrial electron flux, thus limiting the supply of ATP for Pgp, multidrug
  resistance related proteins (MRPs) and breast cancer resistance protein (BCRP).
  Overall these events increase the intracellular retention and toxicity of chemotherapeutic
  drugs (d).
