caption: Proposed Pathway for FabX-dependent UFA formation in H. pylori.In H. pylori,
  UFA synthesis proceeds from a pathway branch at the decanoyl-ACP stage. FabX dehydrogenates
  decanoyl-ACP to trans-2-decenoyl-ACP plus FMNH2 and isomerizes the trans-2-decenoyl-ACP
  intermediate to cis-3-decenoyl-ACP. FabF is required for the elongation of cis-3-decenoyl-ACP
  to allow this intermediate entry into the fatty acid synthesis cycle to produce
  the long-chain UFAs of the phospholipids.
