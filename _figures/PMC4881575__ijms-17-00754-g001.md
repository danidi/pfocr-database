caption: miRNA network regulating cancer metabolism. The shift to aerobic glycolysis
  in cancer cells may be driven or enhanced by miRNAs via regulation of signaling
  pathways and/or transcription factors. miRNAs could regulate cell metabolism by
  modulating the expression of glucose transporters (GLUTs), enzymes involved in glycolysis,
  the TCA cycle and lipid synthesis plus their storage in lipid droplets (LDs). The
  activation of the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway or HIF-and MYC-dependent transcription by
  means of the suppression of inhibitory miRNAs or the upregulation of oncogenic miRNAs
  supports increased glycolysis and inhibits the TCA cycle. Conversely, AMPK signaling
  inhibits mTOR, and p53 suppresses glycolysis and increases mitochondrial metabolism.
