caption: Intratumoral synthesis of E2 and its inactivation by sulfate conjugation
  from circulating steroid hormone precursors in cancer cells. E2 is synthesized from
  E1S in the sulfatase pathway, and from DHEA-S in the aromatase pathway. E1S and
  DHEA-S are taken up from the circulation by transporters, such as those from the
  OATP family encoded by SCLO. In the sulfatase pathway, HSD-17β1 generates E2 from
  E1S, while E1 and E2 are inactivated by sulfonation by SULT1E1. Sulfonated estrogens
  do not bind to the ERα or ERβ. Aromatase generates E2 from androgenic precursors
  via the aromatase pathway. E2, 17β-estradiol; -S, sulfate; E1, estrone; STS, steroid
  sulfatase; SULT1E1, estrogen sulfotransferase; ER, estrogen receptor; DHEA, dehydroepiandrosterone
  sulfate; HSD-17β, 17β-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase; OATP, organic anion transporting
  polypeptides; SLCO, solute carrier for organic anions.
