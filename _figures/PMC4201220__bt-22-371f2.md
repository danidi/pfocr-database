caption: Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathways and a strategy for the phenotype-based drug
  identification using C. elegans mutants. (A) Without signaling (absence of ligand),
  negative regulators (CKIα, GSK3β, Axin, and APC) phosphorylate β-catenin, and then
  ubiquitinated and degraded by the proteasome. (B) With signaling (presence of ligand),
  stabilized β-catenin in cytoplasm is translocated into nucleus and activate the
  expression of target genes. (C) Genetic pathway for control DTC fate specification
  (see text for explanation). (D) Wild-type hermaphrodite has two DTCs. lag-2::GFP
  reporter is strongly expressed in DTCs (see green). (E) Loss of Wnt/β-catenin signaling
  affects DTC fate specification. (F) Activation of Wnt/β-catenin signaling contributes
  to extra DTC formation. (G) Strategy for the phenotype-based identification of drugs
  that either inhibit or activate Wnt/β-catenin signaling using C. elegans mutants.
