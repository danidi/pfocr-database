caption: Growth factors stimulation induces the PI3K/Akt/mTORC1 pathway. CK2 can enhance
  the PI3K/Akt/mTORC1 signaling pathway by phosphorylating and activating Akt. Therefore,
  the CK2 inhibitor, silmitasertib, can result in mTORC1 inhibition. Akt inactivates
  tuberous sclerosis complex 2 (TSC2) through phosphorylation, leading to TSC1/2 separation
  from the lysosomal membrane. Rheb and Rag activation can activate and recruit mTORC1
  on the lysosomal membrane, and thereby stimulates protein synthesis and cell growth.
