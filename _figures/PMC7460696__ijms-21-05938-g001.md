caption: Overview of lysophosphatidate (LPA) signaling pathway. Extracellular LPA
  is produced from the enzymatic action of autotaxin (ATX) on lysophosphatidylcholine
  (LPC). LPA is degraded by lipid phosphate phosphatases (LPP)1–3 into inactive monoacylglycerol
  (MAG). LPA signals through at least six known G-protein coupled receptors (with
  three sub-units) to mediate its downstream cellular effects, which are dependents
  on the coupling and/or subunit type.
