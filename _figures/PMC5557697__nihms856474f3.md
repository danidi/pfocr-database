caption: Model depicting the Cdc42-mediated regulation of EGF receptor interactions
  with c-Cbl. EGF receptor activation stimulates GDP-GTP exchange on both Ras and
  Cdc42, resulting in the formation of Cdc42•GTP-Cool-Cbl complexes and signaling
  through the Ras-ERK pathway. When the Cdc42-Cool-Cbl complex disassembles, Cbl can
  then bind to the EGF receptor and is phosphorylated, thus enabling Cbl to function
  as an ubiquitin E3 ligase that catalyzes receptor ubiquitination and degradation.
