caption: WNT plasma membrane signaling in PDAC. (Left panel) In the absence of FZD-LRP5/6
  signaling (lack of WNT ligand and/or inhibition by extracellular DKKs or SFRPs),
  β-catenin is targeted for degradation. GSK3β activity also impinges other signaling
  pathways and controls ubiquitin-mediated degradation of proteins. (Right panel)
  Augmented by RNF43 mutations or RSPO inhibition of RNF43, WNT ligand signaling re-localizes
  and inactivates the destruction complex to stabilize β-catenin, increasing its nuclear
  translocation and co-transcriptional activity. GSK3β sequestration in multivesicular
  bodies (MVB) prevents substrate phosphorylation, promoting WNT-STOP, WNT-MTOR, and
  further pathway crosstalk (i.e., de-repression of MAPK/ERK). Thus, upstream WNT
  pathway inhibitors (PORCN inhibitors, FZD receptor blockers, and FZD decoy receptors)
  mediate additional actions divergent from downstream WNT pathway inhibitors targeting
  β-catenin in the nucleus (i.e., PRI-724). Created with BioRender.com.
