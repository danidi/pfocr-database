caption: Figure 1. Lymphoma cells may regulate intranodal Vγ9Vδ2 T-cell expansion
  via a BTLA- and HVEM-dependent signaling pathway. (A) Isopentenyl pyrophosphate
  (IPP) produced by cancer cells can be recognized by the Vγ9Vδ2 T-cell receptor (TCR),
  hence stimulating Vγ9Vδ2 T-cell proliferation. Lymphoma cells expressing the herpesvirus
  entry mediator A (HVEM) inhibit the trasnduction of Vγ9Vδ2 TCR-elicited signals
  by interacting with B and T lymphocyte associated (BTLA), hence impeding Vγ9Vδ2
  T-cell proliferation. (B) Monoclonal antibodies directed against BTLA or HVEM allow
  Vγ9Vδ2 T cells to proliferate in spite of the presence of HVEM+ lymphoma cells.
