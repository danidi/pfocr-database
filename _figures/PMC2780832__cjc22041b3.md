caption: Mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) intracellular signalling pathway.
  After binding of growth hormone (epidermal growth factor, platelet-derived growth
  factor, fibroblast growth factor-2, insulin-like growth factor-1) to its tyrosine
  kinase receptor, the receptor becomes activated as a protein tyrosine kinase (PTK).
  PTK undergoes autophosphorylation at multiple tyrosine residues, which interacts
  with signalling proteins, in turn activating Ras, a low molecular weight G-protein.
  Ras leads to activation of MAPK through protein kinase cascade. Thereafter, MAPK
  induces the transcription of proto-oncogenes (c-fos, c-myc, c-myb, b-myb, ras)
