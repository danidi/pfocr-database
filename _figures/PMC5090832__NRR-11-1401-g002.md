caption: Proposed signaling mechanism underlying the neuroprotective role of tPA.tPA
  enters the cell where it indirectly activates cellular signaling mechanisms, including
  Janus kinase 2 (JAK2) and/or mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR). tPA may function
  through the JAK2 pathway initially to activate signal transducer and activator of
  transcription 3 (p-STAT3) and promote gene transcription, and/or activate the mTOR
  cascade for eventual activation of p-S6, and/or converge onto another pathway to
  enhance expression of ribosomal p-S6. When activated, both the JAK2 and mTOR pathways
  may converge and contribute to p-S6 phosphorylation, which in turn may contribute
  to enhanced protein translation and improved neuronal survival.
