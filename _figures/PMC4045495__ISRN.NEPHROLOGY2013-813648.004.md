caption: 'GC-C-dependent signaling pathway of guanylin peptides in the intestine.
  Guanylin (GN), uroguanylin (UGN), and heat-stable enterotoxin of E. coli (STa) activate
  GC-C which increases intracellular concentration of cGMP which in the enterocytes
  causes the following: inhibits the Na+/H+ exchanger type 2 (NHE2) and activates
  protein kinase G type II (PKG II), activates protein kinase A (PKA) directly or
  indirectly by inhibition of phosphodiesterase III (PDE III) followed by increase
  in intracellular cAMP concentration. PKA and PKG II activate Cl− and HCO3− secretion
  via activation of cystic fibrosis transmembrane regulator (CFTR) followed by an
  activation of the member of Slc26 family which exchanges bicarbonate for chloride.'
