caption: . JAK-STAT signaling as an anti-inflammatory target. JAK-STAT signaling mediates
  the brain inflammation induced by LPS, IFN-γ, ganglioside and thrombin. Curcumin
  activates SH2-containing phosphatase 2 (SHP2), while rosiglitazone and 15d-PGJ2
  increase the expressions of SOCS1 and SOCS3. SHP2 and the SOCS proteins are typical
  negative feedback molecules of the JAK-STAT pathway.
