caption: Simplified relationship between ROS-induced redox signaling and mitochondrial
  dynamics. ROS stimuli are recognized by redox sensors (top), which transduce the
  signal to their respective effector kinases (center). Phosphorylation by these kinases
  can either inhibit mitochondrial fusion (red) or stimulate fission (green) proteins
  that results in the shift of the overall balance from elongated to fragmented morphology
  (bottom). As indicated, Ras/ERK MAPK signaling involves multiple kinases. Redox
  sensor for mammalian cyclin C pathway has not yet been determined.
