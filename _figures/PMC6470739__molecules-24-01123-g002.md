caption: The antioxidant signaling pathway regulated by quercetin. Environmental factors
  increase the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS). The mitochondrial electron
  transport chain (mito ETC) is another robust source of intracellular ROS generation.
  Quercetin can regulate the enzyme-mediated antioxidant defense system and the non-enzyme-dependent
  antioxidant defense system. It can also regulate signal pathways such as NRFB, AMPK,
  and MAPK caused by ROS to promote the antioxidant defense system and maintain oxidative
  balance. ROS in turn enhance the production of APE1/Ref1 and the activation of several
  signaling events including p53-mediated apoptotic events, MAPK pathways, the NF-E2-related
  factor (NRF2)-mediated activation of genes containing antioxidant response element
  (ARE), and NF-κB [,,,,].
