caption: Inhibiting T cell activity causes anti-PD-1/PD-L1 immunotherapy resistance.
  After PD-1 blockade, the secretion of cytokines, including TNF and IF-36, causes
  T cell gene alterations, which inhibits cytotoxicity, promotes TIM-3 and VISTA inhibitory
  checkpoint expression, up-regulates CD38, and promotes ATRA secretion and binding
  to adenosine receptor and adenosine inhibition of T cell activation. The deletion
  of PTEN in tumors activates the PI3K/AKT pathway through multiple routes, including
  phosphorylation of Akt and activation of S6K1, to promote PD-L1 expression and inhibit
  T cell activation
