caption: β‐Catenin‐dependent pathway. a Off‐state. In the absence of Wnt‐signaling,
  β‐catenin is phosphorylated and degraded through the Axin/APC/GSK‐3β/CK1α destruction
  complex. Cytoplasmic active β‐catenin is maintained at a low level, and total β‐catenin
  is mainly involved in cell‐extracellular matrix adhesion. In the nucleus, the expressions
  of Wnt target genes are shut off by binding the transcription factor TCF/LEF to
  co‐repressors Groucho and HDAC. b On‐state. Upon binding of Wnt ligand to FZD and
  LRP co‐receptors, a signaling cascade is initiated. LRP and Dsh are phosphorylated
  by CK1α and GSK‐3β and the Axin/APC/GSK‐3β/CK1α complex are disrupted, thereby resulting
  in the impaired degradation and accumulation of β‐catenin in the cytoplasm. Then
  accumulated active β‐catenin translocates to the nucleus to serves as a transcriptional
  co‐regulator of Wnt target genes through forming an active transcription factor
  complex with TCF/LEF as well as CBP/p300
