caption: A. Canonical Wnt signaling results in inhibition of the “destruction complex”
  and β-catenin-dependent transcription (left panel). In contrast, Wnt/PCP signaling
  results in mutual antagonism between Fzd/Dvl and Vangl/Pk complexes, leading to
  cytoskeletal rearrangements (right panel). B. The asymmetric distribution of core
  PCP complexes in the developing Drosophila wing results in consistent distal localization
  of the wing hair throughout the tissue. C. Wnt/PCP pathway activation within individual
  breast cancer cells results in asymmetric localization of core PCP components in
  a manner analogous to PCP in polarized epithelial cells. These complexes respectively
  promote and restrain actin cytoskeletal dynamics, resulting in directed migration.
