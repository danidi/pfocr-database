caption: Diagram of ergot alkaloid biosynthesis in the C. fusiformis wild type (WT)
  and a transformant carrying the CpcloA gene. In the C. fusiformis wild type the
  pathway ends at elymoclavine due to a nonfunctional CfcloA gene. In addition to
  elymoclavine, agroclavine accumulates, perhaps due to feedback inhibition. In the
  C. fusiformis transformant carrying a functional CpcloA gene, lysergic acid and
  elymoclavine accumulate. The absence of functional lpsA and lpsB genes in C. fusiformis
  apparently precludes the synthesis of ergopeptines in this transformant.
