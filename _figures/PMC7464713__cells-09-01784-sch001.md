caption: Scheme of the molecular basis of the Wnt pathway. A central role in indirect
  mechanotransduction is played by Wnt signaling. In the inactive state, the key component
  β-catenin (β-cat) is constantly degraded by the destruction complex consisting of
  AXIN, adenomatous polyposis coli (APC), glycogen synthase kinase 3 (GSK3) and casein
  kinase 1 (CK1). In the active state, initiated by Wnt-binding to the receptor complex
  Frizzled (Fz) and Low-density lipoprotein receptor-related protein (LRP), the recruitment
  of Dishevelled (DVL) occurs. This further recruits components of the destruction
  complex, such as AXIN. Subsequently, β-catenin accumulates in the cytosol and translocates
  into the nucleus where it acts as a co-activator of transcription factors (TCF).
  β-catenin is regulated by the inhibitor of β-catenin and TCF-4 (ICAT).
