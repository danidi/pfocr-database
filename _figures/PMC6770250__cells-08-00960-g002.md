caption: TGFβ signaling in cholangiocarcinoma (CCA) progression. TGFβ activates SMAD-dependent
  (canonical) and SMAD-independent (non-canonical) pathways in order to evoke transcriptional
  programs that, ultimately, regulate physiological responses in CCA. Consistent with
  its dual role in cancer, TGFβ can either prevent CCA progression, by inducing apoptosis,
  or enhance CCA progression, by promoting EMT, migration, invasion, and suppression
  of the immune system. Many coding and noncoding TGFβ-target genes can mediate the
  effects of the pathway during CCA progression and a number of them could potentially
  serve as drugable targets and biomarkers of CCA. Current therapeutic approaches
  aim at targeting components of the core pathway, such as the TGFβ ligands and the
  type I TGFβ receptor (TβRI).
