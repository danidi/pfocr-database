caption: Prevalent mutations in acute myeloid leukemia (AML) potentially affect HIF
  levels in cancer cells. Mutations of genes encoding isocitrate dehydrogenase (IDH)
  1 and 2, which are mutated in 10–15% of AML, inactivates PHD to suppress HIF hydroxylation.
  FLT3 mutations (found in 25–35% AML cases) stimulates PI3K/AKT pathway to enhance
  HIF translation. NPM mutation, which was found in 30% of AML with normal karyotype
  (NC), reduces p14ARF, and HIF inhibitors. TP53 mutation reduces expression of MDM2,
  an alternative E3 ubiquitinylation ligase E3 for HIF. This in turn may reduce HIF
  ubiquitinylation.
