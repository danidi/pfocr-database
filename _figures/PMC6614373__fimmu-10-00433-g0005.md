caption: Signaling crosstalk at the IκB module. (A) The non-canonical NFκB signaling
  pathway can cross-regulate the canonical NFκB through NIK-IKK1-mediated degradation
  of IκBδ. High-molecular weight complexes of IκBδ trap RelA:p50 dimers in the cytoplasm
  to limit inflammatory NFκB activity. (B) Stimulus-responsive transcription initiation
  factors regulate the synthesis of IκBα. GCN2 and PKR phosphorylate eIF2α to inhibit
  IκBα synthesis in response to UV light and IFNβ, respectively. In contrast, phosphorylation
  of eIF4E by MNK1 stabilizes the IκBα mRNA. IFNγ promotes the proteolysis of IκBα/ε
  by upregulating the 11s cap of the immunoproteasome.
