caption: Hedgehog signaling pathway in cancer stem cells. The Hedgehog pathway plays
  a key role in stem maintenance, self-renewal, and regeneration of CSCs. The secreted
  Hh protein acts in a concentration- and time-dependent manner to initiate a series
  of cell responses, such as cell survival, proliferation, and differentiation. After
  receiving the Shh signal, the transmembrane protein receptor PTCH relieves the inhibition
  of the transmembrane protein SMO, which induces Gli1/2 to detach from SUFU and enter
  the nucleus to regulate downstream gene transcription. During activation of the
  Hh pathway, some proteins (IL-6, IL-27, Fbxl17 (F-box and leucine-rich repeat protein
  17), PPKCI, RARα2, RUXN3, SCUBE2, HDAC6 (histone deacetylase 6), USP48, CK2α, WIP1,
  GALNT1, VASH2 (Vasohibin 2), BCL6, FOXC1 (forkhead box C1), and p65), microRNAs
  (miR-324-5p, miR-122, and miR-326), and the long noncoding RNA HDAC2 are involved
  in the Hedgehog pathway to affect CSC growth
