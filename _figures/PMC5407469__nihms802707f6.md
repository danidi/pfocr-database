caption: Ethanol can perturb excitatory balance by altering astrocyte calcium signaling
  and the clearance of neurotransmitters. Ethanol modulates receptor-mediated responses
  in a pathway specific manner, and may directly increase Ca2+signaling in a subset
  of astrocytes. Ethanol also suppresses adenosine-uptake via the astrocytic equilibrative
  nucleoside transporter 1 (ENT1), leading to increased levels of adenosine and activation
  of purinergic receptors (A1AR, A2AR, P2XR) resulting in indirect modulation of glutamate
  and GABA clearence. MR=muscarinic receptor, 5-HTR=serotonin receptor. See text for
  further details.
