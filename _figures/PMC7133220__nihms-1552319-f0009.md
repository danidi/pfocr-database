caption: Neratinib via inhibition of MST3/4 causes Rubicon-dependent phagocytosis
  and in parallel, neratinib activates ATM-AMPK signaling that causes autophagosome
  formation. Collectively, this results in the cathepsin-dependent degradation of
  mutant K-RAS. Atorvastatin via reduced prenylation and sildenafil via increased
  PKG-dependent phosphorylation of K-RAS also independently act to lower mutant K-RAS
  levels. As part of this process at the plasma membrane, neratinib reduces PAK1 phosphorylation
  that leads to dephosphorylation of the PAK1 substrate Merlin/NF2. Dephosphorylated
  Merlin facilitates activation of LATS1/2, and active LATS1/2 phosphorylate YAP and
  TAZ. Phosphorylated YAP and TAZ leave the nucleus, preventing them from acting as
  transcriptional coactivators.
