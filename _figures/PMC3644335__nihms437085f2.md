caption: 'A. In enterocytes, the trafficking pathways of cholesterol are multiple:
  cholesterol intake from micelles via NPC1L1 (pathway I); cholesterol synthesis via
  HMG-CoA reductase (pathway II); plasma LDL uptake with LDL receptor (LDL R; pathway
  III); plasma HDL uptake with SR-BI (pathway IV); Cholesteryl ester (CE) formation
  via ACAT and efflux via chylomicrons (pathway V); and free cholesterol (FC) efflux
  via ABCG5/8 (pathway VI). B. Schematic representation of C. parvum-infected cells
  showing hypothetical pathways of cholesterol that could be diverted by the parasite.
  Source I is micellar cholesterol (MC); source II is cholesterol synthesized de novo
  on host ER (ER-C); and sources III and IV are exogenous cholesterol derived from
  plasma LDL or HDL, respectively. hPM, host plasma membrane; FO, feeder organelle.'
