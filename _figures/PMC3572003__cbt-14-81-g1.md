caption: Figure 1. Schematic representation of the glucose metabolism in cancer cells.
  Cancer cells increase the uptake and metabolism of glucose by regulating key transporters
  and enzymes (shown in red font) involved in glycolytic pathways. Key oncogenic pathways
  are shown in green and key tumor suppressor pathways are shown in purple. The oxidative
  branch of PPP is required in nucleotide synthesis and dihydroxyacetone phosphate
  pathway is critical for lipid synthesis.
