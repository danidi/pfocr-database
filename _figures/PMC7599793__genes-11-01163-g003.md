caption: Role of Wnt/β-catenin signalling in gut tube patterning. After gastrulation,
  the definitive endoderm (DE) forms into the primitive gut tube along the anterior–posterior
  axis. This gives rise to the foregut, midgut and hindgut domains. Wnt/β-catenin
  signalling acts by inducing midgut and hindgut development in the posterior axis.
  Foregut formation occurs when Wnt antagonists secreted from the anterior endoderm
  suppress Wnt/β-catenin signalling, allowing for subsequent liver development.
