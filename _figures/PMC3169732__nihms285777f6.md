caption: Schematic diagram illustrating the proposed signaling pathway involved in
  cigarette smoke extract-induced increase in expressions of inflammatory genes in
  airway smooth muscle cells. Cigarette smoke extract activates the TLR4/MyD88/TRAF6
  signaling leading to the formation of a complex encompassing c-Src, p47phox and
  NADPH oxidase. The activated NADPH oxidase then promotes generation of reactive
  oxygen species (ROS), which in turn initiates the activation of redox-sensitive
  signaling pathways (e.g., MAPKs and PI3K/Akt) and transcription factors (e.g., AP-1
  and NF-κB) and ultimately induces the increase in expressions of various inflammatory
  genes (modified from ; ).
