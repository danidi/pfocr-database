caption: Second messenger signals at the leading edge of polarized ameboid cells ().
  Shown are polarized RAW macrophages illustrating (A) the ruffling leading edge in
  a phase contrast image, (B) the local Ca2+ signal revealed by YFP-PKCα recruited
  to the leading edge membrane in a wide field fluorescence image, and (C) the local
  PI(3,4,5)P3 signal revealed by GFP-PKB/AKT-PH domain recruited to the leading edge
  membrane in a wide field fluorescence image. (D) Schematic overview of parallel
  Ca2+ and PI(3,4,5)P3 (PIP3) signals that activate PH and C2 membrane targeting domains,
  triggering recruitment of signaling proteins to the leading edge membrane (; ; ;
  ; ; ; ; ; ; ; ; ). The resulting bilayer-associated signaling pathway includes a
  positive feedback loop that controls actin and membrane remodeling essential for
  leading edge structure, stability, dynamics, and directed cell migration (; ; ;
  ; ; ; ; ; ; ; ).
