caption: Functional association between mutp53 and mevalonate pathway-associated oncogenic
  proteins (Ras, RhoA, YAP/TAZ). Activated mevalonate pathway promotes prenylation
  and activities of Ras and RhoA, leading to their translocation into the plasma membrane
  and activation of the ERK and YAP/TAZ signaling pathways, respectively. Nuclear
  translocated ERK and YAP/TAZ promote transcription of downstream target genes involved
  in cancer cell proliferation and migration. Additionally, mutp53 increases activities
  of Ras, RhoA, and YAP/TAZ signaling through protein-protein interactions (BTG2,
  NY-F) and enhancing recycling of integrins/EGFR.
