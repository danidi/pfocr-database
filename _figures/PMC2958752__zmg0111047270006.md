caption: Cartoon showing the pathway of cardiac fibrosis inhibited by E2/ERβ. ET-1
  and AngII stimulate TGFβ1 production in fibroblasts, inducing myofibroblast formation,
  SMAD phosphorylation via JNK activation, and SMAD trafficking to the nucleus. Nuclear
  SMAD is required for collagen gene up-regulation and enhanced protein production
  as well as other vital protein(s) production, leading to fibrosis. E2/ERβ inhibits
  these events via cAMP/PKA signaling to block ET-1- and AngII-induced myofibroblast
  formation and JNK activation by TGFβ1.
