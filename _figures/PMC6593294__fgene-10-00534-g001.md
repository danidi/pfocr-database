caption: 'Possible pathways underlying the DLL1/Notch signaling pathway defect. Two
  main pathways have been shown to be involved in osteoblast differentiation: Runt-Related
  Transcription Factor 2 (RUNX2) (OMIM 600211)/Receptor Activator of NF-kappa-β Ligand
  (RANKL) (OMIM 602642) and TGFβ Bone morphogenetic protein (BMP). Mothers against
  decapentaplegic homolog (SMAD) further activates the RUNX2. BMP2 (OMIM 112261) and
  BMP9 (OMIM 605120) are major regulatory factors in (; ) modulating the osteogenic
  differentiation of bone mesenchymal stem cells, advancing mineralization, increasing
  adherence, and facilitating the activation and expression of several markers associated
  with osteogenesis (). Among the main regulators affecting these pathways are WNT/β-catenin
  and HEY1/2 (OMIM 602953 and 604674, respectively). It has been reported that RUNX2
  is upregulated by the WNT/β-catenin pathway. Moreover, it has been shown that RUNX2
  is downregulated by the Notch signaling pathway. Alternatively, Notch upregulates
  hairy-and-enhancer-of-split-1–7 (HES1–7) and HEY1/2. These play a regulatory role
  downstream in the process by activating BMP, in addition to the negative feedback
  on RUNX2 (; ; ). The Notch signaling pathway further orchestrates the process of
  osteoblast differentiation through direct upregulation of RUNX2, BMP, and JNK, which
  activate the SMAD1 receptor by phosphorylating the linker region (; ; ; ; ; ). Green
  arrows indicate the upregulation of the targets. Red arrows indicate the downregulation
  of the targets. Small blue, up and down arrows reflect the results in the affected
  siblings.'
