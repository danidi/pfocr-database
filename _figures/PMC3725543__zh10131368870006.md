caption: 'Proposed mechanism of FAK-mediated growth of C2C12 cells in response to
  IGF-I. A: IGF-I induces phosphorylation/activation of FAK. FAK facilitates in activation
  of the phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt signaling pathway by inhibiting
  TSC2, ultimately resulting in activation of mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR),
  and subsequent activation of S6K1 and 4E-BP1, 2 key regulators of muscle protein
  synthesis. In FAK-KD cells (B), cell growth is inhibited through reduced activation
  of mTOR, 4E-BP1, and S6K1, secondary to reduced inhibition of TSC2. FAC, focal adhesion
  complex; P, phosphorylation.'
