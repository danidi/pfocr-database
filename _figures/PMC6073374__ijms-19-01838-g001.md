caption: Mechanistic target of rapamycin (mTOR) signaling pathway. mTOR is part of
  two different complexes, mTOR complex 1 (mTORC1) and complex 2 (mTORC2). mTORC1
  is activated by the phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K)/AKT signaling pathway,
  and its downstream effectors activate cell growth, lipid synthesis, and metabolism,
  whereas it inhibits the autophagy. This complex can be inhibited by rapamycin. mTORC2
  activates AKT, thus activating also mTORC1. Furthermore, mTORC2 activates the metabolism,
  cytoskeletal organization, and cell survival.
