caption: β‐catenin promotes the fibroblast‐to‐myofibroblast transition and EMT through
  the activation of the TGF‐β1/Smad2/3 signalling pathway. In general, TGF‐β/Smad2/3
  signalling is initiated with TGF‐β‐induced phosphorylation of the cytoplasmic signalling
  molecules Smad2/3. The phospho‐Smad2/3 (p‐Smad2/3) combines with β‐catenin and translocates
  to the nucleus, where it will modulate the transcription of several target genes
  by binding with cyclic AMP‐responsive element‐binding protein (CREB)‐binding protein
  (CBP) to DNA sequences. Our findings suggest that β‐catenin promote both basal and
  TGF‐β1‐induced phosphorylation of Smad2/3.
