caption: Schematic representation of TRIP12, ARF, and P53 pathway. On the left, TRIP12
  controls ARF and indirectly P53 ubiquitination degradation by proteasome, while
  TRIP12 participates in cell cycle progression [,,,]. On the right, ARF is protected
  from TRIP12 when bound to nucleophosmin (NPM)/nucleostemin (NS) and localized in
  the nucleolus. Moreover, in the nucleoplasm, TRIP12 is sequestered by different
  proteins (in the presence of NS or TRADD and in high level c-MYC condition), blocking
  its interaction with ARF. ARF binds and inhibits MDM2, which leads to P53 accumulation
  and cell cycle arrest.
