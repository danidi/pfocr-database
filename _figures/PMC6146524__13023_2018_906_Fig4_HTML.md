caption: Galactose metabolism, glycosylation and inflammatory cascades in galactosaemia.
  a In galactosaemia, the Leloir pathway is disrupted with the absence of GALT, leading
  to an increase of Gal-1-P and disruption of UDP-hexose turnover. b Increased Gal-1-P
  levels may lead to cellular toxicity and ER stress, with competitive inhibition
  of glycosyltransferases and inhibition of UDP-hexose pyrophosphorylases. T [, –].
  Disrupted glycosylation may lead to an upregulation of glycan synthesis genes. Abnormal
  glycosylation of IgG may lead to an activated immune conformation [] and increased
  expression of anti-inflammatory genes. Leptin expression, influenced by abnormal
  glycosylation, cross signals with the HPG axis and gonadal function [, ]
