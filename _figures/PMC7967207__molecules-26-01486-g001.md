caption: Tannic acid (TA) acts as a repressor for multiple proteins essential in several
  oncological signaling pathways. TA inhibits SMAD-dependent gene transcription in
  response to TGF-β, therefore inhibiting the transcription of TGF-β target genes.
  TA also inhibits VEGF/VEGFR, inhibiting a major angiogenesis signaling pathway in
  cancer. Moreover, TA inhibits the expression of SOX2 gene and inhibits EGF/EGFR
  signaling pathway and consequently cell growth and proliferation.
