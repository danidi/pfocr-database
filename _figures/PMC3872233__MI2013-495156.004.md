caption: Toll-like receptor 4 signaling pathway in alcohol consumption. Ethanol promotes
  the translocation of lipopolysaccharide from the gastrointestinal lumen to the portal
  vein, where it binds to the lipopolysaccharide-binding protein. In Kupffer cells,
  lipopolysaccharide binds to CD14, which combines with TLR4 activating multiple cytokine
  genes. TLR4 are activated by MyD88 dependent or independent manner, leading to secretion
  of TNF-α or IFN-β.
