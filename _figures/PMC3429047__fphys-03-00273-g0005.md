caption: A simplified illustration of the canonical Wnt signaling pathway. (A) In
  the absence of Wnt ligand stimulation, β-cat is located within the “destruction
  complex”, phosphorylated by GSK-3 and CK-1α at Ser33 and adjacent serine positions,
  which leads to proteasome-mediated degradation process. (B) Following Wnt ligand
  stimulation, the destruction complex disassembles, resulting in free β-cat accumulation.
  It then enters the nucleus and forms the bipartite transcription factor β-cat/TCF,
  which leads to the stimulation of Wnt target gene expression. Lithium is an inhibitor
  of GSK-3, while cAMP signaling and insulin are able to stimulate β-cat Ser675 phosphorylation.
  Thus, in certain cell lineages, β-cat/TCF can also mediate the effect of lithium,
  cAMP, and insulin on Wnt target gene expression.
