caption: Translational control by ERK and mTOR. Activation of β-ARs during synaptic
  stimulation promotes translation initiation through ERK and mTOR pathways. mTOR
  phosphorylates and inhibits 4E-BP2 (4E-binding protein 2), releasing eukaryotic
  initiation factor 4E (eIF4E) from repression by 4E-BP2. eIF4E assists translation
  initiation by binding to eIF4G to form the initiation complex eIF4F. mTOR also activates
  S6 kinase (S6K), which phosphorylates ribosomal protein S6 to increase synthesis
  of translation regulatory proteins such as eukaryotic elongation factor 2 (eEF2),
  poly(A) binding protein (PABP), and S6 itself. ERK may cross-talk with the mTOR
  pathway via ribosomal S6 kinase (RSK), phosphoinositide-dependent kinase-1 (PDK1),
  and protein kinase-B (Akt). Diagram is simplified.
