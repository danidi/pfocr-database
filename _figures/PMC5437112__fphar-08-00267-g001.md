caption: Illustration of autocrine and paracrine signaling pathways in colorectal
  cancer. Diagram shows how numerous oncogenic signaling pathways, including Wnt,
  PDGF, c-MET, and KRAS, converge to effect epigenomes that facilitate pro-neoplastic
  (“dedifferentiation”) gene transcription. Possible points of epigenetic interventions,
  potentially reversing dedifferentiating epigenomes, are shown. For example activation
  of epithelial-to-mesenchymal transcription factors (e.g., Twist) leads to whole-genome
  chromatin remodeling and phenotypic plasticity, which can be inhibited or reversed.
