  phenotype presented as classically activated M1 (red boxes and lines) and alternately
  activated M2 (yellow boxes and lines), based on currently published research. Grey
  “glycolysis” textbox indicative of homeostatic metabolism in M0/M1/M2. Colored textboxes
  indicate energetic investment in metabolic pathways during polarization (M1/M2).
  Solid lines indicate metabolic pathways of interest to polarization (M1/M2; simplified
  for overall view). Dashed lines indicate pathogen-derived metabolites or pathogen-directed
  metabolites of host-pathogen metabolic interaction. Yellow stars on pathogen indicate
  effects of benefit to survival of the pathogen and red explosive shapes on pathogen
  indicate effects of harm to pathogen survival.Source: Original art by co-author
  Tyler Lawton; pathway model based on referenced research in body of review article.'
