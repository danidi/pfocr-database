caption: The Notch and Wnt signaling pathways. (A) The Notch pathway. A schematic
  depiction of the proteins in the core Notch signaling pathway. Upon ligand-receptor
  interaction, the Notch intracellular domain (NICD) is released and forms a ternary
  complex with MAML and CSL (a DNA-binding protein) in the nucleus to control downstream
  gene activation. (B) The canonical Wnt pathway. The Wnt ligand is modified by porcupine
  in the ligand-producing cells and interacts with the Frizzled (FZD) receptor. The
  ligand-activated FZD and Low Density Lipoprotein Receptor-related Protein (LRP5/6)
  receptors downregulate the activity of the destruction (Dishevelled (Dvl)/Axin/Glycogen
  synthase kinase 3β (GSK3β)) complex, leading to accumulation of β-catenin (β-cat),
  and its localization to the cell nucleus. In the nucleus, β-catenin cooperates with
  T-Cell factor/lymphoid enhancer factor (TCF/LEF) to regulate gene expression.
