caption: Apoptotic cell death by a TG2/calpain/Bax-dependent mechanism involving both
  caspase-dependent and caspase-independent pathways. Solid arrows indicate apoptotic
  cell death signaling pathways induced by DH-II-24-mediated PDT as identified in
  this study. Dotted arrows represent signaling pathways demonstrated previously (,
  , ). Intracellular ROS (rather than intracellular Ca2+) are major mediators of the
  cytotoxic response to DH-II-24-mediated PDT (). DH-II-24-mediated PDT results in
  apoptotic cell death potentially through mitochondria-dependent death pathways that
  are triggered by TG2 activation. TG2 activation facilitates calpain-mediated Bax
  translocation to the mitochondria, where Bax may cause the collapse of the ΔΨm.
  The loss of ΔΨm allows for the release of cytochrome c (Cyto c) and AIF and subsequently
  induces apoptotic cell death through a synergic effect. MOMP, mitochondrial outer
  membrane permeabilization.
