caption: 'General view of Wnt signalling pathway. (A) In absence of Wnt signalling,
  APC forms a ’destruction complex’ with Axin, Casein Kinase (CK1), Glycogen Synthase
  Kinase 3 (GSK3) that leads to hyperphosphorylation of beta-catenin, and consequently
  degradation. (B) Wnt stimulation leads to the inactivation and/or dissociation of
  the destruction complex. In this case, Wnt ligand binds to a Frizzled (Fz)/LRP5/6
  cell-surface receptor complex. This Wnt complex leads to stabilisation of hypophosphorylated
  beta-catenin, which enters the nucleus and interacts with transcription factors
  (TCF/LEF proteins) to activate gene transcription. PM: Plasma membrane. ECM: Extracellular
  matrix.'
