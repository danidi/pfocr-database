caption: Adenylating enzymes can operate on diverse substrates, enhancing the potential
  for identifying new examples of luciferase activity. a) The plant Arabidopsis thaliana
  encodes enzymes related to fatty acyl-CoA synthetases that operate on bulky intermediates
  in the jasmonate biosynthetic pathway. For example, the enzyme At5g63380 can catalyze
  the conversion of 12-oxo-phytodienoic acid (OPDA) to OPDA-CoA; b) Fridericia earthworms
  possess an as-yet unidentified ATP-dependent luciferase activity based on a structurally
  distinct luciferin. The site of oxidation is shown in red.
