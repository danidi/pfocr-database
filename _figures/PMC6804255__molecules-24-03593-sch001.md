caption: 'Biosynthesis of glucoraphanin, its hydrolysis to form the isothiocyanate
  sulforaphane, and metabolism of sulforaphane. The highly reactive isothiocyanate
  sulforaphane is produced in plants as an inert precursor, the glucosinolate glucoraphanin.
  Its biosynthetic pathway originates from the amino acid methionine and proceeds
  in three stages: (i) methionine side chain elongation by two methylene groups (a);
  (ii) formation of the core glucosinolate structure (b); (iii) secondary modification
  of the glucosinolate side chain (c); Upon disruption of the plant tissue integrity,
  glucoraphanin comes into contact with myrosinase, which catalyzes the hydrolysis
  of glucoraphanin to give sulforaphane (d); In mammalian cells, sulforaphane is metabolized
  through the mercapturic acid pathway, and can also undergo an interconversion to
  erucin (e).'
