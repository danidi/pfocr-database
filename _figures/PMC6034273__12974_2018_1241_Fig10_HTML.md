caption: The interaction among MCP-1/CCR2 signaling, TLR4, and GSK3β in ethanol-induced
  neuroinflammation. EtOH may stimulate TLR4 and possibly CCR2, and activate the downstream
  effectors, such as TRIF and MyD88. Active TLR4 may also activate GSK3β which may
  further stimulate TLR4 pathway. As a result, the activation of TRIF, MyD88, and
  GSK3β stimulates transcription factors which upregulate the expression of proinflammatory
  cytokines and chemokines (e.g., TNF-α, IL6, and MCP-1). The released MCP-1 interacts
  with CCR2 and further activates TLR4 and GSK3β; the positive feedback loop intensifies
  the neuroinflammation that is toxic to neurons in the developing CNS
