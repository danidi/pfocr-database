caption: The classical IL-6/STAT3 signaling pathway in cancer cells. IL-6 binds to
  the membrane-bound IL-6 receptor α (IL-6R) and IL-6 receptor β (also known as gp130).
  The IL-6/IL-6R/gp130 complex activate the phosphorylation of JAKs, followed by STAT3
  phosphorylation and activation. Growth factors, such as FGF, IGF and EGF, can also
  phosphorylate STAT3 by binding to their cognate membrane receptors. Then, phosphorylated
  STAT3 forms a homodimer and translocates into the nucleus to bind to the promotor
  region of target genes and activates target gene transcription
