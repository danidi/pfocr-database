  DNA damage. E2 acting at membrane-localized ERα rapidly activates PI3K/AKT to phosphorylate
  and dissociate TopBP1 from ATR in the setting of DNA damage. This leads to inhibition
  of ATR activity and downstream signaling to the G2/M checkpoint and p53-induced
  p21 transcription. Signaling through PI3K and AKT also inhibits Chk1 activity by
  phosphorylating claspin.
