caption: Proteins derived from genes differentially expressed with temperature feature
  in important signaling pathways. Pathway analysis with the Kyoto Encyclopedia of
  Genes and Genomes () (KEGG) revealed where proteins derived from both κB genes (green)
  and non-κB genes (orange) are involved in key signaling pathways. Genes that were
  down-regulated at 40 °C compared with 37 °C are shown in italics; the remaining
  genes are up-regulated. Where gene and protein names differ, the gene name is given,
  for consistency. These interactions indicate that their differential expression
  of the associated genes could have important implications for future signal transduction
  through these pathways. To avoid bias, only interactions identified using KEGG are
  shown, leading to the omission of the roles of notable proteins such as A20 (TNFAIP3)
  in the NF-κB signaling pathway, and NEDD4L in the Wnt pathway.
