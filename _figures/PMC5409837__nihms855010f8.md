caption: (A) In hiPSC-CMs, receptor tyrosine kinases VEGFR2, PDGFRα, INSR, and IGF1R
  are upstream of pro-survival signaling pathways. (B) Our results suggest that VEGFR2/PDGFR-inhibiting
  TKIs upregulate INSR and IGF1R signaling (phosphorylation) to compensate for the
  loss of VEGFR/PDGFR signaling (phosphorylation). This compensatory effect can augment
  cardiomyocyte survival during TKI treatment via introduction of exogenous insulin
  and IGF1 ligands. We observed an increase in the downstream gene expression of non-canonical
  VEGF-binding receptors and VEGFR pathway members, presumably to compensate for VEGFR2/PDGFR-inhibiting
  TKI-induced loss in upstream VEGFR signaling (phosphorylation).
