caption: Changes in nutrient availability or treatment with low doses of Actinomycin
  D lead to nucleolar (ribosomal) stress. As a result, ribosomal proteins L5 and L11
  bind to and inhibit the E3 ligase function of Mdm2, resulting in p53 stabilization
  and activation. In addition, oncogenic insults, such as aberrant expression of c-Myc,
  induce the expression of both p19ARF and ribosomal proteins. The RP-MDM2-p53 and
  p19ARF-Mdm2-p53 represent two parallel signaling pathways in response to oncogenic
  c-Myc stimulation.
