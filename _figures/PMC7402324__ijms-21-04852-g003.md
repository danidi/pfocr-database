caption: Proposed model of the crosstalk between canonical and non-canonical WNT-signaling
  in melanoma. In the canonical WNT pathway, WNT–FZD/LRP5/6 interaction initiates
  β-catenin dependent signaling. β-catenin translocates to the nucleus to drive the
  transcription of target genes. This is critical for early steps of transformation
  when melanocytes bypass senescence and start to proliferate, thus promoting first
  the radial then vertical growth of melanoma. An increase of WNT5A that activates
  non-canonical WNT-signaling inhibits β-catenin-signaling and enhances the invasiveness
  of melanoma cells crucial for metastatic spreading of melanoma. Green and red arrows
  indicate increase and decrease, respectively.
