caption: Upon glucose depletion, the level of fructose 1,6-biphosphate (FBP) derived
  from the glycolysis pathway decreases. The glycolytic enzyme aldolase, which normally
  binds to the v-ATPase complex in FBP-loading state on the lysosome surface, becomes
  unoccupied upon FBP deprivation. The dissociation of FBP alters the interaction
  between aldolase and the v-ATPase-Ragulator complex, resulting in the formation
  of a protein complex consisting of the v-ATPase, Ragulator, AXIN, LKB1, and AMPK
  at the lysosome surface and the activation of AMPK. Activated AMPK phosphorylates
  multiple downstream substrates, and several examples are listed.
