caption: Autophagic signaling pathways associated with oxidative stress. External
  OS and other stimuli can activate the PI3K-Akt-mTORC1, Ras-MEK-ERK1/2, and ASK-
  JUK axis [, , ]. Activated PI3K recruits PDK1 (phosphoinositide-dependent kinase
  1) and phosphorylates Akt, inactivating the TSC1/2 (tuberous sclerosis complex 1/2)
  and leading to mTORC1 activation []. mTORC1 is an energy/redox sensor, the function
  of which can be blocked by rapamycin []. It represses autophagy by phosphorylating
  Atg13 and separating it from the ULK kinase complex []. Elevated cytoplasmic ROS/RNS
  can be sensed by AMPK, ATM kinase, and PARP-1 [, –]. AMPK activates autophagy by
  inhibiting mammalian target of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) and directly activating
  Unc-51-like kinase 1/2 (ULK1/2) [, ]. ATM or PARP-1 are able to activate the LKB1
  (liver kinase B1)-AMPK-TSC1/2 metabolic pathway to repress mTORC1 or directly activate
  ULK1/2 through phosphorylation of its serine groups, upregulating autophagy [, ].
  As well as OS levels, AMPK also responds to cellular AMP/ATP levels, endoplasmic
  reticulum (ER) stress, and CaMKKβ (calmodulin-dependent protein kinase kinase-β)-related
  signaling [, , ]. p53 exhibits paradoxical autophagic regulation in OS, as nuclear
  p53 positively enhances autophagy through TSC1/2-dependent pathways, whereas cytoplasmic
  p53 seems to do the opposite []. DAPK, BNIP, Bax, and Puma all function to break
  down the interaction between Bcl-2/Bcl-xL and Beclin-1, normalizing the essential
  formation of the class III PtdIns3K complex [, , ]. UVRAG (ultraviolet irradiation
  resistance-associated gene) also positively regulates this PtdIns3K complex, while
  Rubicon counteracts its effect [].
