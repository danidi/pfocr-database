caption: Signaling pathways that modulate cardioprotection in CMs. Mechanical and
  oxidative stress produced in disease or exercise can induce a complex anti-apoptotic
  and hypertrophic response in CMs that converges on the RhoA and PI3K/Akt signaling
  pathways. Stimulation of GPCRs through ligands triggers RhoA-dependent actin polymerization
  and nuclear localization of MRTFs to activate transcription of MRTF/SRF target genes.
  Expression of targets such as miR-486 and CCNs, as well as exercise-induced mechanical
  tension can activate the Akt response to contribute to cardioprotection. RhoA can
  also modulate the Hippo/YAP signaling pathway to regulate proliferation and stress
  responsive genes during both development and disease.
