caption: Proposed inhibitory feedback loop for MSC osteogenesis. We propose that phosphoinositide
  3-kinase (PI3K)/Akt activation is a key signaling event required for the induction
  of osteogenesis in human bone marrow MSCs. As osteogenesis progresses, Cav-1 is
  upregulated as a negative feedback mechanism to suppress continued osteogenesis.
  PI3K/Akt signaling is one pathway that Cav-1 suppresses within caveolar membrane
  rafts to achieve this end, and as osteogenesis progresses more phosphorylated Akt
  (p-Akt) is translocated to this subcellular compartment
