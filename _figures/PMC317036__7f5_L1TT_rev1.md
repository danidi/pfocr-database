caption: Model showing the possible relationship of dTOR with the insulin pathway.
  The unexpected finding that hypomorphic mutations of dTOR, but not null mutations
  of chico, strongly inhibit dS6K activity may be explained by a two-threshold model.
  The threshold for dS6K inactivation by dTOR is low, whereas that for dS6K inactivation
  by the Inr pathway is high. Thus, a low level of PI3K signaling from Inr may be
  sufficient for dS6K activation in the absence of Chico. Dashed arrow indicates indirect
  or unclear interaction. Solid arrow indicates direct interaction.
