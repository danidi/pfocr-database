caption: PPAR-controlled fatty acid transporters CD36 and FATP import fatty acids
  into the hepatocyte (). These fatty acids can then be stored as triglycerides or
  undergo ω- or β-oxidation, principally controlled by PPARα (). Inhibition of the
  PPARα pathway can lead to steatosis. LXR induces lipogenic transcription factor
  SREBP-1c, which acts by up regulating lipogenic genes including FAS, ACC, and SCD-1
  (). PPARγ also induces these genes (; ). PXR promotes lipogenesis through inhibition
  of PPARα and activation of PPARγ (). FXR inhibits lipogenesis through SHP induction,
  which blocks LXR. SHP also inhibits CYP7A1, the rate-limiting enzyme for the formation
  of bile acids (). ACC, acyl-coA carboxylase; AOX, acyl-coA oxidase ;CPT-1, carnitine
  palmitoyl transferase 1; CYP4A1, cytochrome P450 4A1; CYP7A1, cholesterol 7α-hydroxylase;
  FAS, fatty acid synthase; FATP, fatty acid transport protein; FXR, farnesoid X receptor;
  LXR, liver X receptor; PPAR, peroxisome proliferator activated receptor; PXR, pregnane
  X receptor; SCD-1, stearyl-coA dehydrogenase; SHP, small heterodimer partner; SREBP-1c,
  steroid regulatory element-binding protein.
