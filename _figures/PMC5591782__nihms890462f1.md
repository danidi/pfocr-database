caption: PI3K pathway is activated in response to several extracellular stimuli resulting
  in the activation of downstream serine/threonine kinase, mTOR. mTOR associates with
  its binding partners and phosphorylates the translation repressors 4E-BPs, which
  consequently dissociates from eIF4E. Free eIF4E subsequently associates with eIF4A
  and eIF4G forming the “eIF4F translation initiation complex”. eIF4F consequently
  binds to m7G cap structure via the cap-binding protein eIF4E, promoting translation
  of numerous key oncogenic proteins. Mitogenic and stress signals can also activate
  eIF4E via components of the MAPK pathway including ERK and p38 MAPK. Both converge
  to activate MNK1/2, which bind to eIF4G and phosphorylate eIF4E at ser209 resulting
  in translation initiation. Drugs targeting cap dependent translation include (a)
  mTOR or dual PI3K/mTOR inhibitors; (b) eIF4E inhibitors; (c) inhibitors of eIF4E-4G
  interaction; (d) eIF4A inhibitors; (e) MNK degraders or inhibitors; (f) Inhibitors
  of eIF4E and Me7 G-capped mRNA interaction.
