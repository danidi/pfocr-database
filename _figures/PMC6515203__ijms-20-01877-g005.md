caption: Schematic representation of main signaling pathways activated by growth factor
  signaling through receptor tyrosine kinases (RTK) and the importance of Mg2+. Ligand-induced
  dimerization triggers transphosphorylation of tyrosine residues located in the receptor
  chain, resulting in activation of RTKs. Intracellular signaling cascades activated
  by RTKs include the MAPK pathway, the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway, the JAK/STAT pathway,
  the PLCγ/PKC pathway and the Src pathway. The functional response of these signals
  plays an essential role in the regulation of many physiological processes. Dotted
  arrows indicate activation through the cell membrane; Solid arrows indicate activation
  of target protein; T indicates inhibition.
