caption: The TGF-β signalling pathway. Binding of an activated TGF-β ligand to TGFR-2
  recruits and activates TGFR-1. This, in turn, phosphorylates Smad2 and/or Smad3
  (R-Smads), which then form complexes with Smad4 (Co-Smad) and translocate into nucleus
  to regulate the transcription of various target genes. Smad7 (I-Smad) inhibits the
  pathway through various mechanisms, including mediating the degradation of TGFR-1,
  inhibiting phosphorylation of Smad2/Smad3 or inhibiting the formation of the Smad2/3-Samd4
  complex. In addition to the canonical Smad-dependent signalling, activated TGF-β
  receptors can trigger other signalling pathways including ERK-MAPK, p38-MAPK, PI3K-Akt
  and JNK.
