caption: ANG II signaling and vascular fibrosis. Transforming growth factor (TGF)-β1
  plays a central role in ANG II-induced vascular fibrosis. AT1R can induce TGF-β1
  through an ADAM17/TNF-α pathway as well as through EGFR transactivation leading
  to ER stress and ROS generation. Likewise, AT1R can induce p38 activation and Arg-1
  expression, both of which lead to collagen production. ANG II-induced MR activation
  also induces ASK1 and downstream Nox induction leading to ROS and TGF-β1 signaling.
