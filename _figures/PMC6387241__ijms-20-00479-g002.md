caption: 'Short- and long-term action of ceramides on insulin signaling in muscle
  cells. Ceramides inhibit the insulin signaling pathway in muscle cells by targeting
  in a time-dependent manner two important actors, Akt and IRS1. Ceramides rapidly
  activate either PP2A or PKCζ to inactivate Akt. In the long-term, ceramides induce
  PKR/JNK and/or Prep1–p160 axes to target IRS1. IRS1: Insulin receptor substrate
  1; JNK: c-Jun NH2-terminal kinase; PI3K: Phosphoinositide-3-kinase; PKCζ: Protein
  kinase C ζ; PKR: Double stranded ARN-activated protein kinase; PP2A: Protein phosphatase
  2A. Prep1: Pbx regulating protein 1. Dotted arrows: indirect action; Solid arrows:
  direct action. P: phosphorylation.'
