caption: Deoxynucleotide salvage and de novo synthesis pathways. In the deoxynucleotide
  salvage pathways, thymidine (dThd) is phosphorylated by thymidine kinase 1 (TK1)
  in cytosol and by thymidine kinase 2 (TK2) in mitochondria. In contrast, deoxycytidine
  (dCtd) is phosphorylated by deoxycytidine kinase (DCK) in cytosol and by TK2 in
  mitochondria. The purine nucleosides are phosphorylated by DCK in cytosol and by
  deoxyguanoside kinase (DGK) in mitochondria. In the de novo pathway, ribonucleoside
  diphosphates are converted to deoxynucleoside diphosphates by ribonucleotide reductase
  (RNR). Equilibrative nucleoside transporter 1 (ENT1) and 2 (ENT2) transport pyrimidine
  and purine nucleosides across mitochondrial membranes. Purine and pyrimidine nucleotide
  carrier 1 (PNC1) and 2 (PNC2) transport deoxynucleoside diphosphates and deoxynucleoside
  triphosphates across mitochondrial membranes.
