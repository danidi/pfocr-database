caption: Signaling pathways in osteoclasts involving V-ATPases. (A) WNT/β-catenin
  signaling pathway. V-ATPases influence WNT signaling through its trafficking and
  activation pathways. The accessory subunit of V-ATPase AP2 (PRR) activates WNT receptor.
  (B) Notch signaling. Acidic endosomes enable S3 cleavage through secretase and NICD
  release in a V-ATPase-dependent manner. (C) mTOR signaling. V-ATPases sense the
  signals of amino acid sufficiency and transfer it to mTORC1. V-ATPases also control
  the off/on switch of the V-ATPase-regulator complex.
