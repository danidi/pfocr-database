caption: Ligand-induced activation of PPARα prevents G1→S VSMC cell cycle progression
  and proliferation by transcriptionally increasing expression levels of the CDKI
  p16, an effect that represses the activity of cyclin D-cdk4/6 complexes to phosphorylate
  pocket proteins. The hypophosphorylated pRB proteins p107 and p130 are recruited
  to the proximal E2F-binding element of TERT gene promoter. The complexes formed
  with p107 and p130 induce active trans-repression of TERT promoter, thereby preventing
  the E2F-1-dependent trans-activation of the TERT promoter. This inhibition of TERT
  transcription will inhibit telomerase activity and thereby prevent VSMC proliferation.
