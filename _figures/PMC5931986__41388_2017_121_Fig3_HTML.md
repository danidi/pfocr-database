caption: Nucleolar response to nuclear or nucleolar DNA damage. DSBs in the nuclear
  chromatin cause Pol I inhibition in the nucleolus through an ATM-dependent signaling
  pathway, which includes NBS1, PARP, and Treacle. Pol I is also inhibited upon direct
  rDNA damage in an ATM-NBS1-MDC1-dependent manner. Inhibition of Pol I transcription
  is most likely responsible for the formation of nucleolar caps and relocalization
  of damaged rDNA to this structures, which are enriched in HR and NHEJ factors, thus
  providing an optimal environment for proper rDNA repair. Notably, cap formation
  was not observed upon nuclear DNA damage is several studies, in spite of Pol I inhibition.
  DDR and associated Pol I inhibition activate p53 through the impaired ribosome biogenesis
  checkpoint (IRBC), NMP1, NCL, ARF, ATM, and ATR. Upon genotoxic stress, Cdc14B is
  released from the nucleolus to the nucleoplasm where it promotes APC/CCdh1-dependent
  Plk1 degradation, triggering a G2-phase cell-cycle checkpoint. Asterisks indicate
  DSBs
