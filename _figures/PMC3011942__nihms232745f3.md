  signal-regulated kinase (ERK). Once activated, ERK phosphorylates cytoplasmic proteins
  and translocates into the nucleus, where it regulates transcription of genes involved
  in cell differentiation, proliferation, and survival.Reprinted with permission from:
  Houben R, Michel B, Vetter-Kauczok C, et al. Absence of classical MAP kinase pathway
  signalling in Merkel cell carcinoma. J Invest Dermatol 2006;126(5):1135–1142.'
