caption: 'Role of E2/ER/WWOX in initiating protective pathways. The pathways include:
  Route I, E2/ER-mediated upregulation of antiapoptotic Bcl-2 family proteins, and
  downregulation of proapoptotic Bcl-2 family members () (see the route in yellow
  line). Route II, Activation of the pro-survival ERK/WWOX and PI3K/Akt signaling
  cascades to block the pro-apoptotic JNK signaling and protect the neural tissues
  from damages () (route in blue). Route III, E2 activates PI3K via ERα and mERs,
  followed by activating Akt to phosphorylate GSK-3β at Ser9 for functional inactivation
  () (route in green). Route IV, The SDR domain of WWOX binds and limits GSK-3β activity
  for neuroprotection () (route in light blue). Route V, Suppression of GSK-3β (e.g.,
  by WWOX) leads to a reduced β-catenin degradation, which is regulated by E2 through
  the ERα/PI3K/AKT/GSK-3β signaling pathway () (route in purple). Route VI, In the
  Wnt/Frizzled signaling pathway, Wnt protein induces the activation of Dvl to block
  the activity of GSK-3β. Without Wnt, β-catenin is subjected to destruction by the
  complex of axin, APC, CK1α, and GSK-3β (). Transiently overexpressed WWOX binds
  Dvl to suppress the Wnt signaling () (route in orange).'
