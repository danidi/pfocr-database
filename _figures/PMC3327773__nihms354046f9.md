caption: Schematic representation of the various pathways involved in the cellular
  interactions of HEDS. HEDS reacts directly with NADPH to produce mercaptoethanol
  (ME) and NADP (Pathway 1). It may also react with GSH directly and/or in a reaction
  catalyzed by glutathione-S-transferase (GST) to produce mixed disulfide (MESSG)
  of glutathione (GSH) and ME (Pathway 2). The mixed disulfide MESSG reacts with GSH
  and produces ME and oxidized GSH (GSSG) by the catalytic action of glutaredoxin
  (GRX) (Pathway 3). The glutathione disulfide GSSG reacts with NADPH and produces
  GSH by the catalytic action of glutathione reductase (GRase) (Pathway 4). The conversion
  of GSSG to GSH i.e. GSH recycling requires NADPH recycling (NADP+ → NADPH) by oxidative
  pentose phosphate cycle (OPPC) (Pathway 5).
