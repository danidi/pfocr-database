caption: The functions of NgBR include regulating cholesterol trafficking via directly
  binding to NPC2, hepatic FFA and TG metabolism through inhibiting LXRα transcription
  in an AMPKα‐dependent pathway. In addition, NgBR facilitates dolichol synthesis
  and further induces protein N‐glycosylation when binds to hCIT and Nogo‐B/NgBR axis
  promotes vascular remodelling and angiogenesis via VEGF‐mediated Akt phosphorylation.
  Particularly, sNogo‐B/NgBR lead to improved diabetic glomerulopathy by dampening
  VEGF‐A signalling and reducing eNOS, Akt and GSK3β phosphorylation. Moreover, NgBR
  regulates tumorigenesis via Ras‐related PI‐3K/Akt and Raf‐1/ERK pathways. Finally,
  NgBR deficiency contributes to PD and inhibits axon outgrowth. Endoplasmic reticulum
  (ER); estrogen receptor alpha (ERα); free fatty acid (FFA); Triglyceride (TG); Vascular
  smooth muscle cells (VSMC)
