caption: After infection by RNA viruses, viral RNA binds to RIG-I and induces a conformational
  change that exposes the N-terminus of RIG-I which binds to unanchored K63 polyubiquitin
  chains synthesized by TRIM25 and Riplet. RIG-I then interacts with and activates
  the mitochondrial membrane protein MAVS, which activates IKK and TBK1 in the cytoplasm.
  These kinases then activate NF-κB and IRF3, leading to the production of type-I
  interferons.
