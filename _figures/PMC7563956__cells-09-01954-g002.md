caption: EPAC1 signalosomes. Upon activation of the β-AR, the β-arr2-EPAC1 complex
  is recruited to the plasma membrane where EPAC1 activates RAP2B and PLCε leading
  to IP3 production. This signaling pathway stimulates in turn calcineurin (CaN) and
  Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent protein kinase II (CaMKII). Once phosphorylated by CaN,
  the nuclear factor of activated T cells (NFAT) translocates to the nucleus to induce
  the transcriptional activation of prohypertrophic genes. CaMKII promotes the nuclear
  export of histone deacetylase (HDACs) and thus the activation of the prohypertrophic
  transcription factor, myocyte enhancer factor 2 (MEF2). PI4P produced in the Golgi
  activates the nuclear complex Epac1/PLCε/A-kinase anchoring protein (mAKAP) at the
  nuclear membrane leading to PKD activation and HDAC export.
