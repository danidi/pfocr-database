caption: 'The MAPK pathway: activators and inhibitors. This picture summarizes the
  main components of the MAPK pathways. Upon membrane receptor activation, or viral
  infection [], Ras family GTPases trigger signaling cascades, leading to the activation
  of ERK, JNK, and p38, which in turn regulate transcription of a plethora of genes
  involved in many cell functions, including regulation of viral infection/replication.
  Inhibitors of specific MAPK proteins, discussed in this review, are shown: U0126
  for MEK1, SB203580 for p38, and PD 98059 for ERK1/2. As discussed in the text, CQ
  and HCQ have the ability to interfere with the pathway at the level of Raf or, downstream,
  on ERK1/2 and p38 activation.'
