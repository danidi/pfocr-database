caption: Effects of CoVs on mitochondrial dynamics and autophagy as a quality control
  axis and the regulatory effect of melatonin.Coronaviruses impacts mitochondrial
  dynamics and mitophagy. Coronaviruses such as SARS-CoV-2 inhibits mitochondrial
  fusion through suppressing deubiquitination of ubiquitylated forms of Mfn1 and Mfn2
  by targeting mitochondrial deubiquitinase (). SARS-CoV promotes proteasomal degradation
  of Drp1 leading to induction of mitophagy (X.-Y. ). Some CoVs elevate the ratio
  of LC3-II/LC3-I leading to the autophagosome accumulation in infected cells ().
  SARS-CoV-2 targets USP30 protein and inhibits Parkin-mediated mitophagy (). Difference
  in the effect of various CoVs on autophagy may result from the fact that autophagy
  inhibits the replication of some viruses but promotes the replication of other ().
  Melatonin regulates mitochondrial dynamics through increasing Mfn2 and OPA1 expression
  and diminishing Drp1 expression (; ; X. ). Melatonin also regulates autophagy/mitophagy
  pathway (). In virus infected cells, melatonin shows different effect on autophagy
  markers including beclin-1, Atg5, Atg12 and Atg16L levels, and LC3-II/LC3-I ratio,
  dependent on the type of viruses and infection time (; ).
