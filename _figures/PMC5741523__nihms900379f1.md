caption: HBV DNA polymerase (Pol) binds to DDX3 to suppress the activation of IRF3
  and, although HBV pgRNA activates RIG-I to induce type III IFN via MAVS, TBK1 and
  IRF3, the binding of HBx to MAVS disrupts the RIG-I signaling pathway. HBcAg can
  activate TLR2 in Kupffer cells to induce the expression of the anti-inflammatory
  cytokine IL-10, and HBeAg can suppress TLR2 and TLR4 signaling in PBMC to inhibit
  the expression of TNF-α. Intracellular HBeAg can also bind to the TIR motif of TLRs
  to suppress TLR signaling.
