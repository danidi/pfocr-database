caption: Schematic illustration demonstrating signaling pathways involved in CCL2-mediated
  neuroprotection in rat primary neurons. CCL2 binding to its cognate CCR2 receptor
  stimulates the PLC/IP3R pathway, which in turn activates TRPC channels, resulting
  in elevation of [Ca2+]i transients. [Ca2+]i elevation then results in activation
  of MEK/ERK pathways, leading to CREB activation and consequently neuronal survival.
  In addition to this, CCL2 binding to CCR2 can also activate another distinct pathway,
  PI3K/Akt/NF-κB, which can also lead to potentiation of neuronal survival.
