caption: 'Five main mechanisms have been proposed for how PD-1 modulates T cell functions.
  PD-1 can: (A) directly antagonize T cell receptor (TCR) signaling by recruiting
  phosphatases to tyrosine-containing motifs in the PD-1 tail [], which can prevent
  LCK-mediated phosphorylation of ZAP70 [], (B) inhibit CD28-induced activation of
  PI3K, leading to reduced AKT and mTOR activation [], (C) inhibit the Ras pathway
  [], (D) induce increased expression of transcription factors including BATF, which
  can directly suppress transcription of various effector genes [], and (E) impact
  T cell motility and the stability/duration of T cell/APC interactions [–].'
