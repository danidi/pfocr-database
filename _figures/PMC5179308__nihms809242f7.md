caption: CKD-induced TNF-α stimulates myostatin production via NF-κB . Myostatin production
  is augmented by Stat3 activation which stimulates the transcription factor, C/EBPδ
  . The increase in myostatin impairs satellite cell function and contributes to the
  loss of muscle mass that is stimulated by CKD ;. Myostatin raises Smad2/3 phosphorylation
  suppressing Akt phosphorylation resulting in activation of the ubiquitin-proteasome
  system (UPS) and muscle atrophy ;. Finally, CKD causes muscle fibrosis because myostatin
  can convert FAPs into fibrocytes.
