caption: Schematic illustration of the tanycyte effects on TRH release in the ME.
  Activation of hypophysiotropic TRH neurons in the PVN (1) leads to TRH release in
  the ME (2). TRH stimulates the TRHR1-dependent Gα q/11pathway, which results in
  activation of the phospholipase C and increase in [Ca2+]i (3). Activation of the
  tanycytic Gα q/11pathway enlarges tanycytic endfeet, which may retain TRH in the
  ME (4) and increases the time for degradation of TRH by the upregulated TRH-DE.
  Overall, activation of tanycytic Gαq/11signaling results in a downregulation of
  the TRH release into the pituitary vessels (5)
