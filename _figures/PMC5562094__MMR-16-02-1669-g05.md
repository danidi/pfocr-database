caption: Schematic diagram of HOXB9 facilitating the formation of a hypertrophic scar.
  HOXB9 activates the MAPK pathway by phosphorylating ERK, JNK and p38, potentially
  by interacting directly with p38. Activated MAPK leads to the transcription of laminin,
  FN and Col1 mRNA, increasing their protein expression levels, which reconstructs
  the ECM and contributes to hypertrophic scar formation. Increased laminin, FN and
  Col1 may in return increase p-ERK, p-JNK and p-p38 levels. FN, fibronectin; Col1,
  collagen type I; HOXB9, homeobox B9; ERK, extracellular signal-regulated kinase;
  JNK, c-Jun N-terminal kinase; p, phosphorylated; MAPK, mitogen-activated protein
  kinase.
