caption: In normal cells, insulin signals through the insulin receptor substrate (IRS)
  to the PI3-kinase/AKT pathway in a manner that inhibits autophagy and increases
  the expression of glucose transporters on the cell membrane. In cells with RP loss,
  an increase of ROS stabilizes p53 and induces phosphorylation of S6 kinase. This
  hyper-phosphorylation of S6 kinase results in degradation of IRS and a decrease
  of insulin signaling to PI3-kinase and AKT. This loss of AKT activation in turn
  releases the inhibition of autophagy and reduces glycolysis.
