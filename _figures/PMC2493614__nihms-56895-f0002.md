caption: Multiple Pathways of TIMP-1-CD63 signaling. TIMP-1 and TIMP-4 interact with
  the tetraspanin CD63, which associates with the β1 subunit of integrin receptors.
  Downstream of CD63, TIMP-1 inhibits both the intrinsic and extrinsic cell death
  pathways through activation of the FAK-PI3K pathway. These effects are mediated
  by activation of the extracellular regulated kinase (ERK). In addition, TIMP-1 inhibits
  cell growth through a mechanism similar to that of TIMP-2, involving suppression
  of cyclin D1 and up regulation of p27Kip1. The net effect is hypophosphorylation
  of pRB and cell cycle arrest in the G1 phase of the cell cycle.
