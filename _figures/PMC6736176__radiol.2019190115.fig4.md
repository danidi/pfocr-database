caption: 'Proposed mechanism of increased hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) cell killing
  and reduced postablation tumor growth with neoadjuvant phoshoinositide 3-kinase
  (PI3K)/mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR)/protein kinase B (AKT) pathway inhibition
  in combination with moderate heat stress and thermal ablation. Top: Incomplete thermal
  ablation of HCC cells induces activation of PI3K/mTORC2-dependent activating phosphorylation
  of AKT at serine (Ser) 473 and threonine (Thr) 308, which promotes cell survival,
  growth, and proliferation mechanisms while inhibiting cell death mechanisms, thereby
  resulting in local tumor recurrence and progression. Bottom: Neoadjuvant dual PI3K/mTOR
  inhibitor BEZ235 prior to thermal ablation prevents thermal ablation–induced activation
  of AKT signaling, which promotes cell death mechanisms while preventing cell survival,
  growth, and proliferation mechanisms, thereby resulting in a reduction in local
  tumor recurrence and progression.'
