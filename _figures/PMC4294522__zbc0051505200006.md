caption: Schematic representation of relaxin signaling pathways leading to activation
  of PPARγ. Relaxin binding to RXFP1 results in a rapid activation of adenylyl cyclase
  (AC), followed by a delayed phase that involves PI3K. This results in elevated cAMP
  levels and activation of PKA. The activated PKA phosphorylates CREB, through p38
  MAPK-dependent and -independent mechanisms. The phosphorylated CREB then increases
  PGC1α gene expression. The elevated level of PGC1α then increases the ligand-independent
  transcriptional activity of PPARγ.
