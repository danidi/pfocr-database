caption: CR3 triggers the Syk/calcineurin/NFATc pathway upon engagement by PGL-I-producing
  mycobacteria to rewire the innate response. rBCG::PGL-I targets the lectin domain
  of CR3 on the surface of DCs, PMNs, and MPs. Dectin-1 and CR3 cooperate to induce
  highly efficient entry of the bacilli. This triggers Syk for translocation of NFATc
  to the nucleus and initiates a transcriptional program to generate an NF-κB-independent
  mediator signature. This preferentially PGL-I-triggered pathway does not depend
  on MYD88 even though both cooperate to induce maximum levels of these mediators.
