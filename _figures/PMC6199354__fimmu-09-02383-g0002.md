caption: Ubiquitin modifications in the WNT/β-catenin pathway. When WNT signaling
  is inactivated, β-catenin is phosphorylated (pβ-catenin) in the cytoplasm by the
  Axin/APC complex and degraded by Jade-1 and β-TrCP. Upon stimulation, WNT binds
  to the receptor Frizzled and the coreceptors LRP5/6, recruits Dvl and inhibits the
  Axin/APC complex, leading to the translocation of high levels of cytosolic β-catenin
  into the nucleus. Fz is ubiquitinated by ZNRF3 and RNF43 and deubiquitinated by
  USP8. Dvl is deubiquitinated by CYLD and USP14. Axin is degraded by RNF146, Smurf2,
  and Smurf1. APC is ubiquitinated by HectD1 and deubiquitinated by USP15. Rad6B and
  EDD ubiquitinate β-catenin; Jade-1 also ubiquitinates β-catenin in the nucleus.
