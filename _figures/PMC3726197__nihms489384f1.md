caption: Signaling axis exploited in the phosphatase and tensin homologue deleted
  on chromosome 10 (PTEN) mutant mouse model of prostate tumorigenesis. Receptor-ligand
  complex formation initiates an intracellular signaling cascade ultimately controlling
  phosphorylation (activation) of the AKT survival pathway. PTEN is a phosphatase
  that acts by cleaving a phosphate group from PIP3,4,5 and effectively down-regulating
  AKT activation. Mutations in PTEN designed to mimic those detected in human disease
  enhance the AKT-mediated phosphorylation of target proteins, functionally contributing
  to a tumorigenic phenotype. Phosphorylation of the androgen receptor (AR) induces
  nuclear localization, binding to the androgen response region (ARR), and transcriptional
  regulation of target gene expression.
