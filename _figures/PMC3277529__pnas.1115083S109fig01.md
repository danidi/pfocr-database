caption: Proposed model for TIMP-1–mediated suppression of the Wnt/β-catenin pathway
  via let-7f in human mesenchymal stem cells (hMSCs). (A) The hMSCs secrete TIMP-1,
  which can bind to the exterior of the cell membrane. This action leads to the degradation
  of β-catenin by a complex containing axin 2. (B) In the absence of TIMP-1, intracellular
  let-7f miRNA accumulates, impeding the translation of its target gene axin 2 and
  allowing the stabilization of β-catenin and subsequent lymphoid-enhancer–binding
  factor-1/T-cell factor-1(TCF/LEF)–dependent transcription of Wnt/β-catenin target
  genes. Consequently, proliferation and differentiation into bone cells are promoted
  in TIMP-1–depleted hMSCs.
