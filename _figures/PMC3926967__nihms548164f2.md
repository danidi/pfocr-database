caption: Ligand binding to the IFNAR and transphosphorylation of TYK2 & JAK1 can cause
  recruitment and phosphorylation of STAT proteins other than STAT1 and STAT2 (e.g.
  STAT-3). In addition, IFNα binding to the IFNαR can trigger phosphorylation of IRS-1
  & 2 and VAV. STAT-3 or IRS-1/2 bind and stimulate PI3K upon phosphorylation to activate
  AKT. Activated AKT block proliferative responses and further signals via the NF-κB
  pathway. NF-κB activation regulates transcription of genes with pro-survival functions
  as well as genes that increase antigen processing and presentation. Phosphorylated
  VAV also activates NF-κB, as well as multiple MAP kinases (MAPK). MAPK activity
  can induce apoptosis, inhibit growth and prevent differentiation. See text for additional
  details and references.
