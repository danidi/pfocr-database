caption: GPCR inflammatory signaling from the plasma membrane. Activated GPCRs couple
  to Gq and/or G12/13 to induce phospholipase C-β (PLCβ)–induced Ca2+ mobilization,
  protein kinase C (PKC) activation, and RhoA signaling. These signaling cascades
  converge on myosin light-chain kinase (MLCK) and result in alterations in the actin
  cytoskeleton, adherens junctions (AJs), and consequent endothelial barrier disruption.
  In addition, some GPCRs signal via Gq-extracellular signal regulated kinases-(ERK)1/2
  to transactivate growth factor receptors to stimulate activation of the janus kinase
  (JAK) and signal transducer and activator of transcription proteins (STAT) pathway
  to modulate transcriptional proinflammatory responses. ERK, extracellular signal
  regulated kinase; JAK, janus kinase; PKC, protein kinase C; PLC, phospholipase C;
  STAT, signal transducer and activator of transcription.
