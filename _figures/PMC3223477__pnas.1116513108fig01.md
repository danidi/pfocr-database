caption: Vitamin D and the macrophage antimicrobial response to TB. In the presence
  of sufficient levels of 25D, activation by TLRs or IFN-γ up-regulates CYP27b1, resulting
  in conversion to 1,25D followed by induction of antimicrobial peptides that kill
  M. tuberculosis. This antimicrobial pathway is blocked when levels of 25D are deficient,
  but can be restored in vitro by 25D supplementation. In HIV-infected individuals
  that are vitamin D-deficient, the lack of IFN-γ production provides an additional
  immune deficiency.
